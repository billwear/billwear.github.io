The Masque Protocol

A disciplined interface for sublimating INFP traits into superpowers that thrive in an ESTJ world.

A masque is more than a mask; it’s both a covering and a performance — a deliberate act of stepping into a role. The Masque Protocol is how I frame my INFP core so it shows up clearly in the checklist-heavy world I work in. I’m not hiding; I’m focusing. I choose which traits to bring forward, which to sublimate, and which to shield.

Motto: Choose the self the moment needs — without losing the self beneath.

Origin & Intent

I’ve lived a life that demanded a “culture of expediency and mercy.” That crucible made me empathetic, resourceful, and quietly determined — but my natural temperament (inward, imaginative, values-driven) doesn’t always translate cleanly into the pragmatic ESTJ environments I inhabit. The Masque Protocol resolves that mismatch. When I “put on the masque,” I activate the disciplines that let colleagues recognize my work as reliable, focused, and effective — without silencing the creativity and values that drive me.

Seven Training Modules

Each book below is a module in the Protocol — a targeted sublimation that converts an INFP tendency into visible leverage.

Module 1

Getting Things Done (David Allen)

Translation, not transformation. I use GTD as a translation layer: externalizing my intuitive leaps into visible next actions. Lists aren’t monuments; they’re living scaffolds I capture in tolerable chunks so I never cross the overwhelm threshold.

Sublimation: ideation → ordered, trustworthy delivery.

Module 2

Deep Work (Cal Newport)

My inwardness is a feature. I carve distraction-free focus blocks where that depth becomes output others can see. Short, repeatable sprints or longer morning blocks — either way, I turn quiet intensity into visible progress.

Sublimation: inner landscapes → sharpened productivity weapon.

Module 3

Atomic Habits (James Clear)

I alchemize inspiration into consistency with tiny, compounding actions. The reward loop is tuned to my temperament: strong, immediate “yes” when I align with intent; quick, clean “no” when I don’t.

Sublimation: passion → predictable rhythm.

Module 4

Behave (Robert Sapolsky)

The op-amp brain metaphor lets me raise the “gain” on circuits I want: I pair desired behaviors with an intense like and dampen distractions with a swift dislike. It’s DIY neuro-programming, grounded in biology.

Sublimation: empathy & electronics → scientific armor for action.

Module 5

Thinking, Fast and Slow (Daniel Kahneman)

I shift gears on purpose. System 1 for “android precision” in routines — fluid, efficient, playful motion. System 2 for typing on phone, studying, or careful writing — slowing down into deliberate clarity.

Sublimation: intuition + reason → radar and compass, by choice.

Module 6

The One Thing (Gary Keller)

I ask: “What’s the one thing such that by doing it, everything else becomes easier or unnecessary?” This lens prevents list-paralysis and keeps my work aimed at leverage.

Sublimation: diffuse curiosity → laser alignment.

Module 7

Essentialism (Greg McKeown)

I prune without apology. Saying no protects the signal. I refine values into boundaries so my best work has room to breathe.

Sublimation: idealism → practical clarity & resilience.

Practice Notes

Capture in Tolerable Chunks

I end capture the moment the list reaches the edge of emotional tolerance. Stopping there preserves momentum and protects focus for the work that matters.

Focus via The One Thing

When choices multiply, I ask the focusing question and pick the highest-leverage target. Everything else waits its turn.

Channel Depth into Deep Work

Priority chosen → block scheduled → friction removed. I enter, execute, exit, and record a visible artifact.

Wire It In with the Op-Amp

Immediate emotional reinforcement — a felt “yes” — raises circuit gain and turns choices into defaults. The wrong moves get a quick “no” and no drama.

Toggle Fast/Slow on Purpose

  • Fast: routine tasks become fluid choreography — “android precision.”
  • Slow: phone typing, careful edits, complex reading — paced, intentional, unhurried.
Bottom line: The Masque isn’t for hiding — it’s for becoming visible in a language the world understands.
