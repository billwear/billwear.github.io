It starts with a digest:
- 

“Better To” is the big idea that’s been brewing in my life lately.  If there’s a general, cohesive force, it’s about this gradient approach.
- 

“The Origin of Better To” just gives a little history, about how perfectionism became a bore and a bother, and why I switched channels.
- 

“The problems I have with GTD” gives a little more flavor to the genesis of “Better To” — motion without clear upward momentum.
- 

“The Priority Paradox” is the keystone to my new idea.  Gradients are good, but knowing which way the gradients are leading you is better.
- 

“The Gravity of Choice” is about being stuck.  We all get stuck at times.  And we all need to find our way out as soon as possible, because stuck is deceptively sticky.
- 

“Let There Be Light” shows how I’m trying to choose the positive gradient.  Not “how to make money,” but “how to better myself.”

Now, I have to refine my course going forward. 

Because the point isn’t just to have a clever framework or a fresh metaphor. The point is to live better—**in motion, in awareness, and in direction**.

“Better To” isn’t a rulebook. It’s a compass. It doesn’t ask, *What’s the most efficient thing?* or *What’s the safest?* It asks something far more dangerous and liberating:

> 

**Which of these options actually makes me more alive?**

That’s the question that terrifies procrastinators and perfectionists alike. Because it means we can no longer hide behind process or purity. It means we have to choose—*not the best*, but *the braver better.*

So here’s where I’m going:
- 

**I’m mapping out my gradients.**  
Not abstract goals, not vague affirmations. Real forces. Emotional, energetic, creative. What drags me down? What pulls me forward?
- 

**I’m tracing the field lines.**  
I want to know which choices are “Better To: Learn” and which are “Better To: Let Go.” Which are actually guiding me, and which are just *familiar gravity wells* I keep falling into.
- 

**I’m writing this in public.**  
Because “Better To” isn’t finished. It’s *becoming*. It’s a live feed of one person trying to reorient around truth, traction, and transformation—not productivity theater.

The stakes aren’t small. If I get this right, I won’t just be more organized or fulfilled. I’ll be *more dangerous to the things that waste me.* More aligned. More whole. More useful to the people who need what I’ve got.

If any of this resonates with you, keep reading. Because what’s coming next is a full navigation system—a way to feel your way forward when life is fogged in, the map is burned, and the only signal you’ve got is your gut saying:

> 

*It’s better to move than to stall.*  
*Better to become than to calculate.*  
*Better to start than to stay clever.*
