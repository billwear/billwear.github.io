Some mornings the flame inside doesn’t just warm you. It points at you. It shows you what you’ve been carrying all along.

The world tells you to fix your weaknesses. The fire tells you to follow your strengths.

And the fire is usually right.

You don’t get to choose every part of who you are. Some of it is simply baked into you — a set of abilities and instincts that refuse to go quiet. They’re not loud, but they’re persistent. They don’t demand attention, but they always return. That’s the fire working beneath the surface.

And your job now is to let it grow.

---

I’ve spent decades ignoring my strongest traits because I thought I had to be a different shape to fit the world. I tried to compensate. To round myself off. To get “better” at the things that drained me. But the fire kept slipping through the cracks. It kept whispering the same message in a dozen small ways: **play to the strengths you were built for**.

That’s what finally clicked for me. I’m wired with six characteristics that seem to show up no matter what I do:
- 

**Dexterity**: the maker’s spark — hands that understand tools, materials, craft.
- 

**Intelligence**: knowing the facts, patterns, languages, systems.
- 

**Wisdom**: knowing when and how to use them with judgment.
- 

**Charisma**: the willingness to lean in, connect, warm the room.
- 

**Strength**: the physical and internal force it takes to lift what matters.
- 

**Constitution**: the endurance to stay upright when life pushes back, even with hurricane force.

These aren’t a role-playing joke to me. They are the contours of the fire I didn’t choose but finally accepted. Once I stopped treating them as random traits and started treating them as *fuel*, everything began to align. Creativity returned. Energy stabilised. Confidence rose. The fire got room to breathe.

And here’s the turning point for you: **Your fire has its own six.** You know at least two of them already. The others keep showing up when you’re not paying attention. They’re not chosen. They’re revealed.

---

A scene from this morning keeps looping in my mind. Star*ng out the window, watching the storm outside gather itself. The sky looked like tin, William Gibson’s dead television channel, shifting like it couldn’t decide what story to tell. 

In that strange half-light, I felt this steady heat inside me — not dramatic, not cinematic, just real. It was the quiet recognition that my strengths weren’t accidents. They were the fire’s fingerprints. And they were asking me to stop pretending I’m unfinished or unworthy or “late.” They were asking me to own what has always been mine.

That’s when it hit me: The fire isn’t here to test you. It’s here to *reveal* you.

---

## For you
- 

Write down the strengths that won’t leave you alone; capture them without judgment.
- 

Anchor a small daily action to one of those strengths to reinforce its flame.
- 

Dedicate one focused hour this week to the skill that already comes naturally.
- 

Notice which tasks drain you fast; pause before committing to them.
- 

Identify the one strength that consistently creates meaning for you.
- 

Remove one obligation today that doesn’t align with your natural abilities.
- 

Trust the instinct that pulls you toward what you do easily and well.

---

Let the fire name your strengths. Then let those strengths build your path.

Burn slow. Build deep. Be the proof.
