*07:48 - foggy, 53°F *

Sometimes I wake up already tired. Sometimes the day has a claim on me before I even stand: I know that doomscrolling into the wee hours has a price. Keep telling myself to stop. Addictive.

Sometimes the truth isn’t profound or poetic — it just hurts. But when I listen closely, there’s a strange steadiness under all that noise. A will I didn’t notice I had, a silent center that is the source of sound, surviving contact with everything around it.

And today, that’s enough. More than enough.

I feel the drag of the morning, the turtle headache from skewed sleep, the half-sleep-ness. Old habits call, the ones that soothe, the ones you don’t brag about.

I feel the weight of family history, the way one person can hold a whole tree together even when the roots are cracked.

And I'm still here, getting up, moving anyway. No rush; no pretense. One foot, then the next.

The world will always try to steer me: expectations, wants, griefs, small detonations of shame. They move in creeping slow motion, while my brain jumps ahead. In my head is a different universe, where the speed of light is the lower limit.

I can only function in this world made of cardboard if I suppress my impatience, moving my mouth and hands in first-gear only. Otherwise it comes out like 6x speed and scares everyone.

This inner world remembers who I am and compensates for the slow, loud parade. Helpful. A lot of useful thinking can get done while I wait with frustration for the next word. And that's the trick, isn't it?

That’s the quiet promise of days like this: I don’t need to be fast when interfacing with this kingdom of turtles: the keys will jam anyway, what’s the point? Just be present.

I pause before getting out of bed. The cat shifts but doesn’t complain, then she catches the drift and hops down. At least one creature on this planet thinks as fast.

The house is dim, the air still cold, and yes, I stand there long enough to feel my hesitation.

I know the funeral will take the whole day, that the grief isn’t even mine, really, stepping into a story I didn’t live. Still, there's strength to make tea.

Sometimes that’s what courage looks like.
