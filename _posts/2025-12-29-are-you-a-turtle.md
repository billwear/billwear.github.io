*Today is Pungenday, the 71st day of The Aftermath in the YOLD 3191, the Day of Planning in the Pull Season.*

### mood

My mood today is greatly influenced by a dream. In this dream, I was trying to program. I don’t know whether someone was auditing me or teaching me or what they were doing, but I kept spinning around looking for the right piece of paper and trying to start the program, start the way I wrote the program correctly so that it looked nice. And then I realized that that’s been something that’s always plagued me. So maybe I’m finding my way to this real problem that I need to solve if I haven’t solved it yet. And that is that I don’t just want to do the work functionally. I want it to be aesthetically pleasing, well-organized, well-structured. I want to give the work form as well as function. I want it to be not just a piece of mechanical code, but a sculpture as well. That’s an interesting observation: code as sculpture.

### energy

My energy is definitely about being addicted to the fair weather. My wife is staying over with her best friend. And last night she texted me and said, “Oh, it’s supposed to be 49 in the morning, so you may want to set the heat, even though the air conditioning is currently running.” I did that before I went to bed, but I woke up at 3, and it was still hot outside, and it was now hot in the house. Turned the air on for a few minutes and then noticed that, oh, it’s going to rain. And after it does, it’s going to drop, which it did. So I let the air conditioning run until it caught up and then turned it back on heat. Luckily, the heat hasn’t come on yet. I’m fonder of cooler rooms. It gives me more energy. I understand that surgery suites and hospitals often use this same trick. Not sure exactly what it is, but I’ll take it. I can always wear more clothes.

### mental wx

Whatever my mental weather should be, it’s currently something along the lines of, “well, I don’t want to get out of bed.” I guess that’s understandable. Christmas season over, end of year, dead time, the time when you usually clean out files and vacuum your databases before starting the next year. Except that I don’t work anymore, so no files to clean out and no databases to vacuum. I’ll take that too.

### actual wx

The actual weather is a freaking guerrilla sniper. I’m telling you, it’s windy outside, getting colder; it rained some yesterday, late, it was kind of a heat wave. It’s something my dad used to tell me, that weather is always “warm up, rain, turn cold.” I’m fine with that, that’s a natural cycle. But I don’t like to have all these little Indian summer, blackberry winter cycles through the winter. But I guess that’s my fault since I live in the southern United States. It’s not going to be any other way, especially on the Gulf Coast.

### loop

And now I have a loop of my dad stuck in my head. I can see him when he said that about weather. So we bought this duplex way back in the late 60s in the poorest section of town. I guess my dad bought it because he could get it cheap. It had basically ten rooms, five rooms on each side, plus a bathroom. And he decided to buy it and create throughways in the front and back rooms and upstairs so you could get back and forth. The throughway in the kitchen area was basically an entire wall. And this giant kitchen table sat between the two rooms. And that’s where we ate. And I can see him sitting at the kitchen table, cigarette in hand, drink in front of him, lecturing me on how weather works. Smart guy, but really mean. I’m not sure that many people were sorry to see him go.

### lingering

But that idea of trying to create something that is both aesthetic and functional at the same time is lingering. That’s why I always struggle with projects. I want it to be both beautiful and incredibly functional. That’s probably why I moved toward Unix, because it had the promise of creating art and beauty with a computer. But that also created this weird loop where I started over again and again and again, trying to get it right the first time. I know the ideas of drafting and kaizen now, and I do sort of follow that path a little better. But I still have this inner drive to start out right, get it right, all the way down. Not just the way it works, but the way it looks. I wonder if anybody else has that kind of a weird mis-demeanor.

### unfinished

There are still a lot of unfinished loops in my life. There are things about the renovation that I’ve started but didn’t really move forward with because of the friction of doing them right. I know that sounds weird, but I think it’s probably universal. Also, I have lots of other projects that I’ve started. I have several websites that have never really fully populated, and I have lots of conflicting ideas that I’ve thrown around at times, like trying to write a sci-fi novel, even on Substack. That’s been a little weird. That might not be the right way to do that. But lots of other things where I’ve started things and sort of let them sit. For a long time, I thought I was flighty and never got back to things, but I realized that, in reality, the reason I can’t get rid of those things is because I’m letting them soak. I’m waiting until the moment when I have a desire to actually solve that problem, or when I find a lower friction, more aesthetically pleasing way of doing it. Sounds weird, I know.

### gravity

Gravity is not being a nice lady today. Don’t know what to tell you. Lots of things pulling me in lots of different directions. A leaden sky the color of colloidal mercury, with the wind blowing and probably some rain still falling, temps dropping. Everything says, just go back to bed and try again at 10 o’clock. And I might do that in a minute, but I just feel like I want to finish these thoughts. Ever had that moment?

She will usually just sit down there forever. Yesterday, I was typing something at the laptop, and she came and sat beside me on the desk. ### creative current

Sometimes I still laugh at myself. Sometimes it’s derisive, and sometimes it’s enjoyment and surprise. Yesterday in church, it occurred to me that we lie to ourselves about God a lot. For example, when we say, when God closes a door, he opens a window. It’s not really true. When God closes a door, he opens the whole garage. Somehow, later on, that struck me as funny. I guess everybody’s like that. We all have our funny side. Some more than others, but we all have it.

### pivot

I guess my pivot for today is the surprise at how many people actually read my blog when I just talk about normal things like this. For several months I’ve tried to write powerful essays that get to the point of things and get people’s attention. And some people seem to like them, but fewer people read them on average. With this just average everyday blog post, I seem to get a lot more interest. I suppose I could hyperanalyze it, but why?

### signal boost

I guess the signal boost here is that when I start to censor myself, when I start to try to make the language fancy and eloquent and memorable, I sort of lose my audience. That ought to tell me something, huh?

It’s the person and ideas that come across, the personality, or the lack of trying to force things. I’ve always had a knack for empathy. Not sure where it came from exactly, but I could probably do the hyper-analysis and come up with an answer. Who cares? Achievement unlocked.
