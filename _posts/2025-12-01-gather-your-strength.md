Woke into a day that already has plans for me.

That’s the thing about this time of year — the “pull” season — when the holidays creep in and everyone suddenly wants a piece of your time. Tasks leaning in. Tools whispering from corners. Projects half-breathing. All of it waiting before I’ve even had a chance to decide who I am this morning.

It’s not dramatic. It’s just real.  
A quiet pressure well above 14.7 PSI.  
Fix me. Sort me. Move. Do something.

Most people never notice the first moment of the day, the one that decides everything. The moment where the story could tip either way. The moment determined by whoever generates more gravity — you, or everything pulling at you.

I felt that moment today.

Substack numbers rising like a faint pulse. A new sewing machine on the way. A scavenged ferrite core that might improve the shortwave antenna or might just waste an hour. The campaign list alive under my fingertips. Long-term health insurance humming in the background like a low engine. Sinus weather. Actual weather. And the d20 sitting there like an oracle that never lies but only speaks in tiny, doable quests.

All of it pulling at me at once.

And the best move I’ve learned? Stay in bed a few extra minutes. Let myself catch up. Tell the truth: I’m still learning how to move through this season of life without getting dragged by it.

That’s the start. Not a heroic charge. Just honesty.

---

And maybe you’re in your own pull season too.

Maybe you’re walking similar steps in a different landscape — sorting meaning from noise, trying to figure out what deserves to live, trying to understand why everything feels urgent and nothing feels clear.

Maybe you’re carrying too many small calls at once. Maybe you’re realizing your energy changes when you stop running someone else’s clock. Maybe you’re discovering your own version of rogue-artificer instinct — the part of you that can make, fix, sense, or shift something when everyone else is still trying to find the manual.

It doesn’t matter what form it takes.  
The pull season shows up differently for everyone, but the heartbeat is the same: something is calling you forward, even if you don’t have the words for it yet.

And here’s the turn:

The pull season isn’t something to endure. It’s something to listen to.

The pull is information. It reveals what wants to live. It clarifies which projects matter. It shows you where your internal gravity is getting stronger.

You don’t fight the pull. You shape it.

## **Scene**

Rain taps the window like static on a shortwave. The ferrite core sits unwound on the bench. The sewing table needs clearing. The campaign list glows faintly on your phone. You pick up the d20 and roll. It clacks, spins, settles. A number. A direction. Your hands move before your doubts wake up. One small thing gets done. Then another. The room shifts. Not because you conquered chaos, but because you recognized the call beneath it.

---

That’s where most people finally feel something click. Because they’re being pulled too.By something that wants space.By something ready to begin, even if it starts small.

And that’s the real truth: The pull season isn’t a threat. It’s the first sign of alignment.

Burn slow. Build deep. Be the proof.
