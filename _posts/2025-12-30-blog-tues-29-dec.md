# personal journal of stormrider

Tue Dec 30 08:19:36 AM CST 2025. Today is Prickle-Prickle, the 72nd day of The Aftermath in the YOLD 3191, the Day of Structure in the Pull Season.  
*You will be held hostage by the Invisible Tribe of They.*

## mood

Today my mood is about saying goodbye to superstition.  I’ve gotten caught up in the idea that everything but my talent as a writer is what makes we write well.  For months now, as I’ve found more interested souls on Substack, I’ve been writing in bed, and using this same formula.  Same routine: hit the head, feed the cat, lie back down and write, and then feel like I’ve done my day.  Had to be on my phone.  Had to be in Emacs, in a journal page I designed and automated.  Then I remembered something: Freshman year of college, I wrote a term paper on the spot, without ever reading the actual play, and was invited to publish my essay in the Southern Journal of Literary Criticism.  Shoulda known then. (To clarify, I actually did read the play’s built-in character list, which basically told the whole story in miniature). This was an in-class term paper as a final, for one of the Grand Dames of the Left Bank who was eighty and still teaching freshmen because she loved it.  Then I just walked away.  I wanted to be a famous chemist, but I already was: I’d aced Organic Chemistry the semester before, something nobody ever did before or since at my college.  Then I wanted to be an Electrical Engineer, partly because of the money, but also because I’d been building my own circuit boards since high school.  Yep, shoulda known.

## energy

My energy today is different.  It comes from a different place. I realized that I’ve been trying to hard to get everything right except the expression of a talent that’s already there — that’s already *been* there since before I can remember.  Lord!  We live in a society where natural talent isn’t good enough: It must be disciplined, refined, polished, and then subjugated to the profit of someone else.  Bullshit.  I’m not afraid to say it any more: utter bullshit.  The natural springs of creative water that bubble up in me every single moment are more powerful than anything that could be bought, coached, or trained.  I have all the energy I need when I stop trying to conform to somebody else’s expectations, and stop trying to get all the impotent, peripheral talismans in the right place. 

## mental wx

So my mental weather is in unknown territory, like I woke up on a planet that rains diamonds but didn’t bring a bucket.  But I did act differently without trying.  Didn’t force myself to write in bed, but also didn’t force myself to get up and do the penance of grooming and housework.  Instead, I found my somewhat-neglected slippers, dug my robe out of the guest-room closet, actually *tied* the string on my pajama pants, and made tea.  Tea with no add-ins, by the way, which isn’t even like me at all, except for the fact that wasn’t me ever, just a superstitious shadow.  And here I sit at my laptop, writing whatever comes to mind without recursive editing or spin-doctoring, with the eloquence filters set to “no bullshit.”  It’s a nice day, internally.

## actual wx

Thankfully, the actual weather is covering for my flighty, slightly schizophrenic self.  It’s just above freezing, where yesterday the AC couldn’t keep up with the heat.  It’s sunny, where yesterday it was gray and cloudy and may have even rained a bit here and there.  Even the heat pump is very confused.  Thanks for taking over the job, God, I guess you knew I was going to give it up today.

## loop

Today’s loop is a bit different.  Okay, it’s a lot different, I admit it.  I have things in mind that I need to do, and their urgency hasn’t altered, things about health insurance and secondary income and expense management and completing the renovation and all those things that dog-pile me every morning.  Except they’re keeping their distance, as if they now know I’m going to get to them in due time, without any buzzer shots (hopefully).

## lingering

There is a sad sort of self-recrimination about my unwillingness to recognize my own talent earlier.  I was always a lot more savvy than I acknowledged, and my empathy and ability to read people is top-notch.  How many times did I correctly describe people’s actual motivations across the dinner table, only to be shouted down as extreme or overboard?  How many times did I turn out to be right, but nobody wanted to bring up the subject then?  I always knew a lot more than I allowed myself to believe I did, always had more skill and talent and chutzpah than I would ever trust.  My bad. Not any more, though.

So what’s unfinished, besides petting my clingy cat?  Well, a whole second life. I’m looking at it that way because nobody knows how long we have here, although people like my dad, who smoked and drank radioactively and gave way to his anger at the drop of a hat, should know what to expect.  We’ve covered that ground, though.  Sewing (very good start yesterday); electronics (got all the tools and components and books); Emacs (my favorite technology, and I’ve got every book and resource I need); and writing (finally understand Ken Atchity’s threefold mind in “The Writer’s Time”). And a house that I can afford to pay the mortgage, and keep the lights on, and keep food on the table, all because of steps I took rather unconsciously to put away money during my career.

## gravity

I guess now the gravity is the weight of all the things I haven’t done yet, because I couldn’t let go of the idea that false supernatural forces and the “way of the world” weren’t fully satisfied, so I couldn’t start.  Man, how many times did I start over?  How many times did I want to go back and re-try because the writing wasn’t beautiful?  How many times did I have to make a plan for taking a shower, for fear I’d get something out of order?  I’m a natural engineer, but my predilection for planning everything, trying to avoid the eventual misplaced hair or speck of dust does not come from that gift, but from someplace more sinister, more driven by everyone in my life who wanted to exploit me: my father, whose escape into addiction was fueled by the fear that his talent wasn’t good enough, and who spent his life mis-using his talents and wanted to make damn sure I didn’t do the same thing; my high-school music teacher, who saw me as teenager who could be extorted into doing what he said, even though it was wrong; bosses down the line, who skillfully exploited my abilities with many languages, human and computer, for their own gain and career goals; lovers of the past who wanted something that they thought I could become, something that would benefit them and relieve them of the need to make an effort.  Luckily, I found my person before it was too late, someone who knows my faults, tolerates them as long as she can, and doesn’t force me into any particular direction.

## anchor

I need more people like her in my life.  People who will quietly encourage me to explore my talents and stop doubting myself.  But I also need to be that person for myself.  It wouldn’t hurt me at all to trust myself a little more, trust the talents I have and the gifts I’ve been given.  The world tries so hard to *source* these gifts.  My gift for writing has been around before I could read, but it’s the *actual* reason I learned to read and write when I was four years old.  Oh, and to the school who pushed me to stop being left-handed?  Well, I won’t say the words, but you can figure it out.

## impedance check

So I’ve developed a passive resistance, or rather, impedance.  The distinction is important, so forgive me the momentary lapse into EE-speak.  Resistance exists everywhere; even a good conductor like copper has some resistance to the flow of current.  Impedance, on the other hand, is *selective*.  Impedance is what happens when a certain component will pass some frequencies better than others, and it’s at the heart of how radio works.  In the last twenty-four hours, I’ve recognized that I have a very high impedance for superstition and nonsense.  I’ve said it before, but never actually *lived it* in this way.  In the language of science fiction, I finally ***grok*** it (thank you ever so much, Mr. Heinlein). So now resistance isn’t as important as what I’m willing to pass or block, which is another way of saying that I’ve stopped questioning my tendency to resist certain things, because I know instinctively that they’re just not my jam.

## idea pressure

The idea pressure is pretty overwhelming.  What can I do if I’ll stop censoring myself to follow the dictates of the invisible tribe called “they?”  Hard to even write it down, but I see myself easily managing four or five hobby threads, in a very profitable way (as side-gigs go), and being reasonable about not letting disapproval get in my way.  

## creative current

And here’s the big part: The creative current is so strong that I have to pass it.  A million amps of intuition and creativity and Things Not Yet Made flowing into me.  Gotta be passed into the world, no choice, otherwise it will fry me alive.  

## pivot

This whole reflection is about a pivot, from trusting in all the little talismans to trusting in the gift.  It’s got more “small step, giant leap” energy than a setting foot on a hundred planets, let alone the moon. 

## signal-to-noise ratio

And the signal boost comes from recognizing that my physical body will tell me when I’m off the beam.  I get weird little feelings at times.  They’re not measurable in vitals or blood chemistry (I’ve tried).  They seem to make me want to stop what I’m doing and switch to something else.  Sometimes it’s just a break, sometimes it’s outright abandonment.  Need to start trusting my squelch more.

## rare value

I guess the rare value I can bring to the world is just saying what’s on my heart.  Apparently there’s something rare and valuable in there, something that I just take for granted.  Isn’t that always the way?  I remember someone asking a very productive and creative person, “Where do you find the time?”  He gave them a surprised look and said, “Where do you lose it?”  I’m beginning to believe that in my case, the gifts I have are so pedestrian that I forget to notice them.  Need to start working more, I guess.

## points of friction

So my main points of friction center around exactly what Ken Atchity alluded to in “A Writer’s Time:” I have talent, but I don’t know how to make it productive, not because I’m spendthrift, but because I don’t acknowledge that it’s a talent.  Gotta get over that, you know?

## today’s pulse

The vein running through this morning reflection is pretty stark and simple: I’m digging for gold with a diamond in my hand.  Duh.

## today’s ember

Stop trying so hard.  Just do what I feel is the right next thing.  Don’t compare it to others, don’t follow superstitious rituals, don’t choose things based on people you idolize or hate.  You do you, or more accurately, me do me.

## what wants to live

Whatever I’m drawn to, that’s the thing.  It’s okay to think about ways to make it productive, valuable, and relatable.  It’s not okay to say, “What would the neighbors think?”

## what can fall away

A lifetime of pulling the wool cap over my own eyes.
