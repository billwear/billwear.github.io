It’s not just *your* fire. Not really.

You didn’t spark it, or conjure it from grit or grind or gumption. You were born with embers in your chest, the stored sunlight of every ancestor that ever shaped a tool, fled danger, or whispered a hard truth without flinching.

You are not the first, but you are the *latest.*

When you’re too tired to try again, but try anyway, that ache in your bones is your inner fire. It's memory without language, momentum **from everyone’s past** whose blood you share, and the people around them who shaped *them*. You are not self-made; but you *are* self-directed, and the generations that come after you will feel the weight of your fire, though they may not know from whence it came.

---

## Communal memory is inevitable

The inner fire never forgets. You might question your worth, and the world might question your relevance, but a hundred years from now, that flame will still be alive, burning brightly, filled with every idea and every thought you ever had. The fire knows only motion, transformation, heat.

It doesn’t measure, it remembers.

And it remembers you.[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ITDp!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9f72e14a-f547-4a77-8d84-779109a9ceda_1024x1024.png)

And it will remember you, down through the ages, in the same way it remembers the last hundred generations that brought you to this moment. You don’t need a blueprint; you have a lineage. You don’t need a breakthrough; you need to remember.

The fire you carry is not pictures on a wall, or a branding strategy, or a passion project, or a hereditary profession. It is the **forge inside you** whose fire was shaped by an unbroken chain of gathered centuries.

---

## A holy purpose

Your hands weren’t designed for doom-scrolling. They were made for craft: lifting, turning, creating, building things and relationships and memories that will outlast you.  They were meant to do rare and valuable things without any promise of reward.

This work feels stupid to explain but essential to begin, something you keep returning to even when no one notices. You say the words in the dark because they feel truer and safer there. You pick up tools you've never seen and find patterns in habits no one taught you, but someone, long ago, lived enough to pass down in marrow.

You are made of pressure and persistence, of loss that didn’t break you and lessons that remade you, long before you were born. Tending the eternal flame in the wind, lit by ancient ancestors who speak through action, not memory. 

**Every time you keep going, you answer a prayer whispered through generations —one that never had words, formed and raised only by hope.**

Your ancestors and their entourage weren’t all brave. But enough of them were. Enough stood and stayed, and passed the spark.

Now, here you are, holding the fire.

Not to put on display or sell, but to *remember what matters,* and make something.

---

## Build the thing

So build the thing. Write the line. Hold the silence. Hammer the nail. Lift the stone. Say the name. Boil the water. Light the lamp.

**Not to impress, but to belong**. You are the latest steward of something ancient.

And the fire in your bones has *been waiting* for you to answer.

Light it.  
Tend it.  
Trust it.

And walk forward, not as someone new, but as someone who *remembers.*
