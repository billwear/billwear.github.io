**First electronics pouch**

So today, I built a carry pouch for my SDR and small antenna, using a piece of old blue jeans that had an intact back pocket. Interfaced a piece of darker denim, sewed three sides and turned. Then did a sloppy job of sewing double-folded bias across the top, because the material as previously cut wasn’t deep enough to sew the fourth side and turn it without closing up the pocket.[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PHBk!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F08341bd8-69b8-4379-9816-b0b0eb5e72ce_1440x960.jpeg)[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aJHs!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5218e6b-d7ed-4e69-8b55-d25d303b879f_1440x960.jpeg)

It has one large pocket for the SDR board itself, and a smaller pencil size pocket to hold an antenna or two.[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ASEG!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb6e4bced-c2ea-4f9d-b8da-48e78629be28_1440x960.jpeg)

The pockets are separated by a straight seam, sewn with matching blue thread.[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PlCm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0c53d208-8ba0-48c7-ac59-4286b551bac4_1440x960.jpeg)

This is iterative design: Rather than doing a whole lot of up-front design, I just built a prototype with whatever skills and knowledge I already have for things like this.  Ninety minutes tops.  Anything longer is second-guessing what hasn’t actually happened yet.  Doing it this way forces out the weak spots quickly, and the design can proceed in Borg-like evolutions.

In other words, it’s slower in the beginning, but it improves exponentially, meaning fewer *total* iterations before you converge.
