title: "In medias res"
date: 2025-07-25
---

Late one Thursday evening, after an hour's impromptu nap on the couch, ignoring the television, I woke up feeling both regret at not having yet squeezed in more meaning and profound doubt in the validity of any meaning at all. In truth, it was an impossible trichotomy without contradiction. In the same cognitive instant, I held John Gott's insight -- that the vast majority of moments are ordinary -- alongside Thoreau's wonder at the “different and distant beings [who] contemplate the same [star] at the same moment,” and Pirsig’s assertion about the diluting effect of description.

In that capsule paradox, I felt both miraculous and insignificant, standing as we do at the center point between cosmic and microscopic. We are small enough to wonder whether viruses live, yet large enough to be equally puzzled by the way galaxies obey the master clockwork of physics. 

And yet there I sat, somehow in that moment weaving together the savage emotions of a beast, the logic of a grandmaster, and the heart of a poet. The experience was as seamless as the sky, yet as textured as a cubist masterpiece.

We walk that middle way every instant, condensing the “big, buzzing confusion” of a flowing quantum reality into a tapestry of wonder and hate and joy and moodiness.  Under an umbrella of stars that mirror the mysteries we’ve only vaguely felt, we eat our lunch, pay our bills, and yearn for things not seen to be made plain. And yet none of these “vast and titanic features” can approach the strangeness of our own being.

As another has said, “It is [our] penchant to confront last what lies nearest. The stars are not so strange as the mind that studies them.” Indeed, the very fact that we do so -- that we have the self-awareness to assert separateness and independence, to recognize our own desires and observations in an apathetic universe, to impose order and meaning on the void -- *that* tendency should spark our deepest curiosity. 

In our hurry to have dominion, we fail to notice the scale and scope of our domain. Rushing to claim the earth and the sky and the last morsel of joy and wisdom, we forget that we are merely clinging to our own abstractions. We never seem to acknowledge the greater miracle in our ability to synthesize a workable common reality from a cloud of uncertainty.

Sometimes, on a perfectly ordinary and miraculous Thursday evening, this hidden complexity shows up in the liminal moment of clarity between waking and sleeping, when you feel outside of time, but hyper-aware of it slipping away.  Simultaneously yearning for meaning and questioning meaning itself is far more wondrous than anything the ultimate answer could teach us. 

Or perhaps that sitting quietly, in ordinary moments, comfortably enjoying an indescribable, tripartite paradox *is* the ultimate answer.
