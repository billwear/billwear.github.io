* personal journal of stormrider 

Thu Jan  1 08:24:05 CST 2026. Today is Sweetmorn, the 1st day of Chaos in the YOLD 3192, a Day of Teaching in the Pull Season. 

*You’re currently going through a difficult transition period called “Life.” *** emologent*** mood

Today, I’m sort of spontaneously ignoring my lifelong “one ring to rule them all” obsession. I’ve always believed that I should have one interface to my world — just one way of organizing things. I’m think I’m figuring out that this doesn’t work for me. It might work for others, but it doesn’t for me. 

I’ve spun around the moon many times over the question of paper or electronic. This year I find myself working with both, without any hint of angst. No clean division between what’s on paper and what’s in my Emacs, just whatever feels right at the moment. That’s an odd place to land for for a die-hard engineer, which maybe, just maybe, I’m possibly not, really, degrees and career notwithstanding.*** energy

You’d expect the first day of the year to be about habits, lists, and checklists. That’s how I used to approach it. My energy today has been way different, all about finding my way organically. I know I want paper in my life because some things just come together better that way. I also know paper can be mirrored in Unix directories and Emacs org files. There’s no need to force a single method, and there’s really no need for both methods to contain everything.  I know that sounds loose and sloppy, but it doesn’t actually feel or move that way.  I actually feel absolutely devoted to finding a way to do all the things that I want to do this year, without needing a moonshot countdown to keep the balloon in the air.*** mental wx

My mental weather feels like a day at the beach in the spring, when the sun isn’t such a threat. I’m looking at a planner page called Bucket List and Goals, and just writing in things in different pre-printed categories and changing the category titles when it’s absolutely necessary to make sense for me. In past years I would have written resolutions with precise dates (which rarely meant much) and then planning the moves at single-breath resolution. “Wow, that was some kind of stupid!” I say, looking back.

This year I just wrote things down. Like I said, I adjusted the built-in categories to fit my life: “give back” became ***Books*** I want to write, “eat” became ***Web***, entertainment got loaded with hobbies like electronics and radio and D&D. I ended up with thirty-seven goals. Will they be finished this year?  I don’t know.  Do I care?  Not nearly as much as I thought I might.  No force, no dystopian discipline. That alone feels healthier than anything I’ve done in very great while.*** actual wx

The weather is still a succession of blackberry winters and Indian summers, now in the same 24 hours. My little ear-ritation didn’t go away with the steroids and nasal sprays the NP prescribed.  Went back yesterday, so she threw some Doxy at it, my weapon of choice.  Still, my six-times-yearly sinus infections are down to one or two, thanks to the surgery last year.  Maybe if I can get the guy to randomly do some surgery on the other nostril, things would get better?  

The cat wanted to go out early, but it was 32 degrees. I told her no. Forty-five is about my lower limit for her outside time, and then only briefly. Fifty or fifty-five is better. I can add coats. She just has the one she’s willing to wear. Taking care of her is part of taking care of managing my world, and definitely a labor of love.  Earlier today, she came asking me to sit in the recliner seat in the living room sofa, so I grabbed my laptop and moved there.  She only came up, once briefly, took a few pets, then got on the floor to watch me.  Eventually, satisfied that things felt normal, she went around the corner into the sitting room to nap in her bed.  

The difference?  This time, I was actually chill enough to understand what she was doing.  My wife’s been staying with her best friend almost 24/7 while her husband’s been in the hospital.  The cat just wanted enough normalcy to feel comfortable.  Chill is good.*** loop

My loops also feel different this year. They always been about what I didn’t do, didn’t do well, or did so badly that it was embarrassing.  I’d always play back these memories at the most inappropriate times, usually under stress, when I should have been motivating myself.  A rolling disaster area. Now, though, my loops are about things I actually started and haven’t finished yet, or haven’t gone deep enough into. And it’s not accusatory at all, just kinda like a little reminder that I need to keep moving forward. That feels like suitable progress.*** lingering

These things-I-want-to-do still linger, but now they have multiple points of existence. Electronic and paper, convenience and actual weight, give my goals some real presence. I think I’m learning that discontinuities and redundancies aren’t a bad thing, really. I could print instead of handwriting, and that would be fine, it’s just the physical nature of the thing.  Once something is physical, once you can hold it, it matters more, to me at least. It becomes real.*** gravity

The gravity here is not trying to be too smart or clever. Overthinking always trips me up, but for some reason, this time, I’m not adding unnecessary superstructure.  I think I’m genuinely asking myself whether I want the *plan* or the *results *to look good.  And it isn’t just about the wasted time trying to prophesy the future.  Somehow, it’s more about letting go of the need for overly-high certainty and vanishingly low doubt. I’m just writing things into the bucket list, without constraining them in any way, really.  It’s a unique feeling that creates a sense of greater trust.*** anchor

Which means the thing I have most to regret is all those years and dollars spent on silly self-help literature, the genre of empty panacea and false hope.  If I had a nickel for every nickel I spent on those, I’d have all my nickels back.  That’s just a convoluted way of saying that my anchor doesn’t need to weigh two tons or hang on an eight-ton chain.  A toothpick on a string is good enough, as long as there’s some certainty and rationality there.*** impedance matching

The frequency of the day is finding the system by feel, not by computation.  I used to want to build an infinite PERT chart for blindingly simple operations.  Now that just looks so silly to me.  Quite a change in perspective.*** idea pressure

There’s pressure, but it’s low, steady, and joyous.  I *know* I can produce things that I *will* be able to trade for money.  I don’t know *how* I know that, I just do.  That’s sort of weird.  Yes, part of me wants to be suspicious of a Pollyanna complex, but it’s not loud enough to dampen my devotion to doing a good job.*** creative current

The current is calm and moving. Writing feels alive, and that makes things feel available and achievable. Again, it’s impossible to source this optimism and enthusiasm, but the trend is clearly there.  Nice.  I’ll keep it.*** pivot

The pivot is toward fit, not perfection, toward what *feels* right over what looks right.*** signal-to-noise ratio

The signal is basically undefined, because I don’t see my unknowns as *noise* any more.  It makes no sense to divide by zero.*** rare value

The rare value here might be permission: permission to use more than one tool, to leave things unfinished (for now) without guilt, to start without making the plan a military operation.  Plans that don’t survive contact with the real world aren’t really plans, are they?*** points of friction

The main friction is still my tendency to overthink structure, and the tension between wanting clarity now and knowing it comes later.  I caught myself a couple of times today feeling overwhelmed at some of the structures I was putting in place, but after all, I’m trying to hold onto thirty-seven goals without being OCD about it.  A certain amount of up-front detail is necessary, so I just took breaks.  I *never* take breaks in planning.  Bravo, dude.*** quest pulse

The pulse is quiet but steady: build a life that supports making things that matter to myself *and* others, not managing systems that give only the appearance of maybe being able to actually produce such things.  It’s actually a bit of breakthrough for me.*** today’s ember

Today’s ember is that bucket list page. One page, thirty-seven items, no deadlines.*** what wants to live

Obviously, this plural approach is here to stay.  Paper and electronic, multiple notebooks, collections of files, all indexed in a seamless and low-friction way.  *** what can fall away

I guess I’ll have to let my precious fall into Mt. Doom.  It’s the only way, Gollum.
