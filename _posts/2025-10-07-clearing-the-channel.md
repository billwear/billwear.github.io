If the morning was about striking the first note, the afternoon is about keeping it clean. You’ve already tuned your signal; now it’s time to clear the channel it moves through.

Noise doesn’t just come from screens. It comes from clutter, half-finished thoughts, background tasks, unread tabs, and the low hum of things you said you’d do but haven’t yet. Every unclosed loop pulls a little bandwidth from your brain.

Take fifteen minutes—no more—and make a *quiet list.*  
Write down everything that’s bugging you, large or small: the text you haven’t answered, the drawer you meant to fix, the file that’s been open since Tuesday. Don’t solve any of it yet. Just capture it.

When it’s out of your head and onto paper, you’ll feel the static drop. That’s not magic. That’s physics. You’ve just lowered the noise floor.

Now pick one item—only one—and finish it completely. Don’t multitask. Don’t optimize. Just close the loop. Then sit still for sixty seconds and notice the silence that follows. That’s the sound of reclaimed signal.

Resonance grows in those gaps. Keep them clear.

---

**Quick steps for the afternoon**
- 

Write the quiet list. Empty your head of loose ends.
- 

Choose one item and complete it start to finish.
- 

Step away from your devices for five minutes when it’s done.
- 

Ask yourself: *Did this make my corner clearer?*
- 

If yes, strike another note.

---

**Mid-day mantra:** *Small completions restore big focus.*
