title: "Your Grandmother's Fire"
excerpt: "Stop trying to sell me back my own wisdom"
date: 2025-06-12
---

Everyone's drowning in revolutionary new frameworks that feel suspiciously familiar.

Mindfulness is now a biohack. Authentic leadership is a methodology. Doing what matters requires a course and a subscription plan.

And deep down, beneath the buzzwords and good intentions, we know the truth: you're just selling me my grandmother's fire with a trademark symbol.

This avalanche of new-speak doesn't enlighten. It erodes. We hear echoes of truths we learned young -- from quiet mentors, long walks, hard-earned lessons -- now spun into slide decks with pastel color palettes.

We used to call deep work concentration. Morning routines were just getting up and doing your chores. Digital minimalism was just not wasting time on nonsense. And rare and valuable meant do what matters most, first.

No frameworks. No apps. Just fire, started the hard way and tended with care.

---

You can feel it in your jaw -- that ancient tension. The little internal flare-up when someone teaches you, for the third time this quarter, that boundaries are life-changing and revolutionary.

You knew this. You always knew this.

But now you're being sold it back, and it stings. Someone packaging inherited wisdom as a product. A sales pitch stapled to something your grandmother whispered while shelling peas, trying to extract more nickels from ancient communal knowledge.

Buy. Buy more. Subscribe now. And be happy.

Alexander Pope nailed it three centuries ago: we think our fathers fools, so wise we grow. Our wiser sons, no doubt, will think us so.

So we smile and nod, and our fire flickers just a little.

Don't let it go out.

---

Here's the hard-earned truth: you don't need a new system. You need remembrance.

You already carry the matches. You already know the kindling. You already have the wisdom, passed down in sweat and scars and lullabies. You don't need a twelve-week program to start your day with intention. You just need to trust yourself.

Principle sixteen: distrust all claims for the one true way -- including the ones that arrive in beautifully designed email sequences with a free PDF. The wisdom being repackaged was yours to begin with. The only thing the package costs you is the suspicion that you already knew.

Because real fire -- the kind you build slowly, the kind that lights generations -- doesn't ask for applause. It just burns. And when the flashy ideas burn out, as they always do, you'll still be there. Kneeling in the dust, tending the embers, building a fire strong enough to last through the night, through the storm, through hours and days and years of living.

---

This truth is not for resale.

Real fire -- built from character, rooted in time-tested wisdom -- can't be ignored and won't be forgotten. Not by your children, not by the world, not by history.

When the winds rise and the lights go out, when the water won't come out of the faucets or the heat's on the blink, people don't go looking for the flashiest flame. They go looking for the person who knows how to light a fire and keep it lit.

That's you.

That's always been you.
