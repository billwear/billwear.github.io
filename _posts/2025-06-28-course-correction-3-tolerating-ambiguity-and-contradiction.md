*How exquisite confusion gets us back on course faster*

> 

“Once on a high and windy hill, in the morning mist….”

That line drifted through my head this morning, and it stopped me cold.

It’s poetic. Romantic. Nostalgic.

But if you know anything about weather systems, it’s also nonsense.

**Mist requires stillness.** It forms when moisture condenses in calm, humid air -- usually in low places, sheltered from the wind. **Windy hilltops disperse mist** before it can even settle. 

That scene? It’s meteorologically incompatible.

And yet it’s *true.* Emotionally true. That impossible weather map captures something no satellite image ever could: **the emotional atmosphere of love.**

---

## Contradiction is human

We’re trained, especially in tech, science, and systems work, to correct contradictions, to resolve ambiguity, to smooth the edges until a thing “makes sense.”

But what if those jagged edges are exactly where we find **real meaning**?

> 

“Do I contradict myself? Very well then, I contradict myself. (I am large, I contain multitudes.)” 

— Walt Whitman

When we deny our contradictions, we fracture. When we embrace them, we integrate.

We are the windy hill and the morning mist. We are joy and grief, doubt and clarity, stillness and motion -- sometimes in the same breath.

---

## Course correction through complexity

This post is part of my “course correction” series, written not from a mountaintop of certainty, but from the trail itself. And here’s what I’ve learned walking it:

**You don’t get back on course by erasing contradiction. You get back on course by making space for it.**

Every time I’ve tried to “fix” myself by logic alone -- by squashing a feeling, over-rationalizing a choice, or denying emotional whiplash -- I’ve only delayed healing.

But the moments I’ve made space for my inner weather systems to clash where the storm meets the stillness? 

Those are the moments I find alignment again.

Not clarity in the clean sense, but something better: A **felt coherence**. A deep exhale that says, *I don’t have to resolve this right now to keep moving.*

---

## The exquisite complexity of being

There are some truths that **can't** be plotted on a straight line or pinned to a whiteboard:
- 

The ache of missing someone you were never sure you loved.
- 

The joy of letting go of something you worked your whole life to build.
- 

The clarity that emerges after you stop trying to define it.

These aren't errors in our emotional code, they’re **proof of depth.** Love, grief, hope, faith: these are *many-splendored things.* Not because they’re tidy, but because they hold contradictions in a single fragile frame.

And when we stop needing to solve those contradictions right away, we start to **move again.**

Maybe course correction isn’t about finding the “right” coordinates, but allowing the compass needle to dance through uncertainty, trusting that it will steady again.

---

## Let yourself be…

Let yourself be mist and wind; joy and despair; disgust and interest; curiosity and certainty. 

Maybe you’re in a season of emotional paradox. Torn between identities, pulled in opposing directions, full of big, exquisite, confusing weather.

Take heart.

You’re not broken. You’re *weathering.*

That’s not a failure of clarity. It’s a mark of aliveness. Let yourself stand still in that impossible moment.

Where black and white don’t make gray, but a beautiful rainbow of bittersweet colors that only you can see.

Where yes and no may be said *together *with confidence. 

Where you can complain about the things you love, but still love them and embrace them with no qualms or hesitation.

Where the very silence you feel is somehow the source of all sound and music and poetry.

And it might be the exact moment your course begins to shift -- quietly, gracefully -- back toward home.
