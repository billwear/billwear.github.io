### **Tuning, Not Turning Away**

The best way to fix your relationship with tech isn’t to quit it.  
It’s to **train it.**

Right now, look at the last feed you opened.  
Ask: *does this amplify what I actually value—or what someone else wants from me?*  
Then cut one source that only sells you noise.

I mean it.  Just drop it; delete it; block it; spam it; throw it out with the mop-water.  
Replace it with one that feeds your craft, your curiosity, or your calm:
- 

a **maker channel** that teaches you to build something tangible (electronics, sewing, woodworking, cooking).
- 

a **writer’s log** that talks about process, not hype.
- 

a **slow science or engineering journal** that values explanation over outrage.
- 

a **long-form interview feed** where people actually finish a thought before being interrupted.
- 

a **local or niche Substack** where someone’s thinking deeply in your domain.
- 

a **music or field-recording stream** that re-tunes your nervous system instead of lighting it up.

It’s not detox. It’s design.  
The tools bend toward the hands that shape them.  
Make yours a tuning fork, not a panic button.
