*On slow starts and ember days*

---

Some days the fire won’t catch.

You sit where the work happens. Same chair. Same coffee. Same habits, same morning air --but nothing flares. 

The spark you’ve taken for a *granted* *wish*? 

Gone missing: no spark. 

No ignition, just fog and weight. Maybe a dream still echoing behind your eyes.

This is that kind of day for me.

And it’s okay.

---

## The Myth of the Constant Blaze

Our culture worships the bonfire. Hustle, output, and glow-up are the currency of social media. If your fire isn’t big and bright and visible from space, something’s wrong.

Real fires don’t work that way.

Ask anyone who’s ever camped out: **fire has moods**. It roars, crackles, settles and sleeps. At times, it's a beacon. Some days it's a single coal under a bed of ash. 

And on those days, what matters isn’t how fast you can reignite — it’s **how you tend the ember**.

---

## The Art of Ember Days

This morning I felt slow and sluggish. After all, it is a Saturday. I was haunted a little by a late-snooze dream that was really a memory dressed as a metaphor: a box of broken parts, a pair of strange glasses, a missing wall.  Myself and a high-school classmate (we once challenged the system in court and won).  On a road trip, stuck with the weight of what hadn’t yet been fixed.

Not the kind of morning where you leap up and write a manifesto.

But I wrote anyway; not for the fire, but for the ember.

Here’s what I’ve learned about mornings like this, when energy is low and clarity is elusive.

---

### Don’t demand flame from coals

Tired doesn’t mean broken, and fog doesn’t mean failure. It just means today isn’t about performance, it’s about **presence**.

Take a breath and sit with the coals. Let the day arrive *as it is*, not as you wish it were.

---

### Choose a small spark

Follow the smallest thing that still glows: one good sentence; a warm line; a note to your future self.

If all you can do is whisper, **whisper honestly**.

---

### Stop pretending this is new

You’ve come through this fog before. You’ve lit the fire again — many times. Don’t reinvent yourself *(again)*. Just remember who you were the last time the fire kicked up.

Because it will.

---

### This is the deeper fire

Anyone can burn bright when the world feeds them fuel. Don’t count on that. Learn how to *tend*, how to find your own fuel.

That’s a rare and valuable art.

In the slowness, the not-yet, the murk, your deeper wisdom *will* show up. It’s when you’re tired that your honesty emerges — not polished and postured, but *present*. 

Sometimes that presence is all someone else needs to keep going. Not heat or clarity, just the quiet confidence to carry the glowing embers to others. 

Someone in a deeper, thicker fog may be praying for those small sparks.  Share them.

---

### **The fire never really goes out**

It banks low and waits  —  and teaches.

The act of tending until you coax light and warmth and power from ash is a holy act, wise and strong. 

So today, if you’re having a slow morning or an emotional hangover, feeling foggy, groggy, weird, or stuck: You're not lost.

You’re just on ember time.

And ember time still burns.
