Didn’t expect the local fauna to broadcast. Not here. Not like this.

It started with a pressure behind the eyes, a soft static bloom, the kind you get when a scanner locks onto a channel it was never meant to receive. Then a thought slid in under the noise. Not language. More like intent wrapped in velvet and claws.

A cat. Small, lean, coded a little rough around the edges. She walked in like she owned the deed. Sat down. Looked at me. And pushed.

**Food. Now. Also: you don’t live here. Not really.**

My people can read telepathy fine. Evolutionary runoff. We can take it apart, run it through the inner filters, watch the emotional vectors unspool in real time. But we can’t send anything back. No transmitters. No resonant glands. Just air and voice. Primitive replies to high-bandwidth demands.

I tried speaking anyway. Local phonemes. Local syntax. She didn’t buy it. Sent back a sharper pulse, needle-clean:

**Talking is noise. Don’t make me do this twice.**

So I complied. First-contact protocol, feline edition.

She ate like a returning landlord demanding proof of occupancy. Walked the perimeter, cataloged scent mismatches, tested gravity with each step. Tail twitching. Ears cycling. Broadcast signatures shifting through a palette of ownership, disappointment, and something like pity.

Apparently she lived here once. Or remembers it like she did. Hard to tell. Telepathy carries ghosts the same way metal carries heat.

### *Side Channel: MarVal Reconstruction*

Spent the rest of the afternoon trying to piece together MarVal in Emacs Lisp. The old gradient engine. Decision calculus the way we used to do it before the fleet standardized AI-run planning units.

It’s been a long time since I touched the underlying math. Grade school, maybe. Back when they still taught us the differential equations by hand, before we surrendered them to soft-silicon tutors. Trying to remember how the slopes stack, how local maxima masquerade as absolutes, how noise makes a field lie to you.

Feels like digging up an old religion.

I’ve got a few scraps — partial derivatives, heuristic biasing, the crude human-style version where you fake the geometry and hope the day doesn’t fold wrong. It’s something. Not enough, but something.

Running the whole thing from scratch here in a text buffer made of ancient Earth Lisp? Might take me a while. Might take longer if the cat keeps overriding my cognitive stack every time she wants dinner.

But this is the job now.

Telepathic fauna.  
Broken planning systems.  
One Emacs frame running on borrowed hardware.

Rebuilding a life one gradient at a time.
