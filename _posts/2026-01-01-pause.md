So in the new year, I'm doing something a little different. 

I have always written my own stuff, I've never really relied on AI to be anything but a smart dictionary. It's useful for that. It's not really a great writer; it definitely doesn't have *The Knack* for essays yet.

And of course, I have to remember that it isn't actually a thinking machine. It's really just a semantic tool. As I wrote a few days ago, it's kind of like things that have come along in the 20th century: cars, radio, television, computers, social media. These are all things that, at some point, some preacher somewhere has declared the work of the devil. 

I'm sure there were demonic declarations before that. I don't know exactly the history of books, but I know that for a long time, the Catholic church didn’t allow just everybody to actually read the Bible. They had to go through the priests, who were educated in Latin, in which the scriptures were written. Books were rare, and almost considered to be of the devil. Or maybe they were; I don't know, because I don't have insight into that history that far back.

Anyway, more to the point, I've decided that I need to give myself some soak time to think about what I'm writing and how I'm writing it -- rather than just publishing it in real time.  Especially since I have finally experienced the truth behind Ken Atchity’s “managing editor.”

And yes, I own that em dash. LGBTQ+ did not invent the rainbow, and ChatGPT didn’t invent the em dash. Get over it.

For this reason, I'm just posting this little thing today. I'm still writing according to my emotional log entry, and translating that into things that others can read and enjoy. But I'm doing it with a one-day lag, so I have time to think about it, study it, figure it out, and then publish it. 

Forgive me this one-day break.
