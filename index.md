# This Terminal Life
You may or may not have used a computer terminal in your life. Doesn't matter -- there's something here for everyone.  For those of you who lean toward the command-line interface (CLI), you know that the CLI strips away the bloat of modern computing so you can actually get things done.

But life requires the same discipline.  We are all terminal, and time is too short for balloons and confetti and doomscrolling.  This is a space for practical living, sharp focus, and plain living, whether you work from the shell prompt or just try to get by on a weekly basis.

## About me

i first used 'nix over 50 years ago, and it's been my passion and my go-to since. i've tried lots of other tools, but they leave me feeling flat and immobile and wanting to get back to the command line.  eventually, i just gave in.

i use a loaded hp elitebook running debian 13, no graphical interface. if i have to use javascript on a site, i just use my phone.  otherwise, i generally use emacs.

## things i do

i retired in late 2025.  since then, i sew (it's a hobby), write books (you can find them on amazon.com), and do a few other odd things to make up the shortfall in my social security.  will work for money.

i'm also a sort of informal historian of systems from the 1980s era. i keep a dictionary of chatronyms (chat abbreviations, mostly from irc, but many have lived on into the world of texting).  i also have an ever-shrinking list of phone numbers that will get you interesting recordings, many of them telephone company intercepts that have survived.

i also have lots of photos, which i'm slowly but surely posting to this website, although most of them aren't that interesting to people who aren't family.

lastly, i have some small tutorials i had previously posted on the web, for general use, and a collection of essays with no particular cohesive theme, just things that strike me from time to time.
