UNIX™ philosophy

the netizen's way

life at the command line

On June 14, 1974, I logged into a UNIX™ system for the first time. I was fifteen. It changed my life.

Over those fifty years, the ideas of UNIX became my personal philosophy: principles I aspire to embrace every day of my life. These principles work for me. I couldn't say how useful they are to others, but I know they've taken me to some interesting places, kept me humble in the highs and lifted me up in the lows.

I don't try to tell others how to live unless they ask. None of us are born the same; why should I?
