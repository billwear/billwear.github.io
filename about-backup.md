# About me

Here are a bunch of lists about me, in case you\'re curious. The
footnotes might be more interesting than the lists, honestly.

## My credo, in alpha order (because they are equally important):

- **Authenticity** Be who you are. *A bent wire can still carry a great
  light.*\
- **Connection** Network. *We were born to connect.*\
- **Curiosity** Trial and error is the only way we learn. *Keep a
  voltmeter handy, so you don\'t become a memorable lesson in failure.*\
- **Ecology** Use what you have, and patch it as you can. *The Next Big
  Thing will be expensive, and what will you do with the old one?*\
- **Focus** Do one thing well at a time. *Multitasking is a lie.*\
- **Honesty** Say what you mean. *Nothing is truer than the truth.*\
- **Foresight** Think ahead, but never worship your plans. *Motivation
  comes from getting started, not the other way round.*\
- **Leverage** Leverage time, resources, technology, and expertise (but
  not people). *A bigger hammer is rarely the right answer.*\
- **Simplicity** Keep it simple. *Better yet, keep it in a kit you can
  take with you.*\
- **Tolerance** Live the Prime Directive, and distrust all claims for
  the One True Way. *Keep your nose out of other people\'s business;
  never judge or imitate others.*

## How I try to apply my credo to my daily life:

- I\'m overweight, but this isn\'t ecologically sound. Overconsumption
  means we have to generate too much food, too much medicine, and too
  many medical events that require travel; too much cloth to cover my
  body, which translates to too many raw materials and too much energy
  consumed to make those clothes. And the list goes on. If I can
  remember this when I\'m loading up my plate, my weight can stabilize
  and I\'ll live a better life.

## Core skills that I\'ve just always seemed to have, not ranked because they\'re all active at the same time:

## The skills that people *thought* I had that nearly ruined my life, until I learned to think for myself:

1.  Academic and scientific prowess: Just because I made all A\'s
    didn\'t mean that I wanted to be a rocket scientist.\
2.  Operatic singing voice: My untrained voice is *almost* good enough
    to make the grade, but it\'s not quite there.\
3.  Mathematical skill: People ask for tutoring all the time, but they
    don\'t realise I do maths by definitions, not mechanics. My
    arithmetic is horrible.\
4.  The ability to charm people: I may seem to be charming, but really,
    I just have it in my head that we\'re all equals and deserve equal
    respect. It\'s not the same thing.\
5.  Making up words and languages: Before I was eight, I had created my
    own words for a few things, and when I\'m in a bad mood, I tend to
    talk in non-words, but it doesn\'t indicate any particular skill.
