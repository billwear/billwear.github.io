Took up sewing maybe ten years ago, but a demanding new job stopped me after four years. My recent retirement restoked the flames. New machine: bigger, more capable; the real thing.

Going back isn’t easy, and the new machine is complex. Lots of automation. No AI, thankfully; don’t need a wiseguy peanut gallery. This kit came with an extension table, so the whole thing’s about thirty inches wide.

Made big plans, but hit a wall. Hadn’t sewn long enough before to lock in good habits. Luckily, I have books. My brother once said that I could learn anything from a book, and said so when I was rebuilding an engine in my garage, using a Chilton’s. No clue, but I can read. Car ran for at least ten more years, running decent when I sold it.

Yeah, two mug rugs. The third one turned out very nice, so I took it to a friend who loves coffee as an early holiday present. But it’s not automatic yet, so more mug rugs in the immediate future.

I’m gonna get good at this. I’m gonna sell handmade goods and do shows. Maybe even be the Bob Ross of sewing. Not negotiable.
