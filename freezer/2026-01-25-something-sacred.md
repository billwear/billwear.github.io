Exquisite, heartfelt moments are Baby’s specialty. 

Eight years ago, three months old and freshly abandoned, our beautiful Bombay Black picked our porch to share her love. She seemed equally hungry for food, warmth, and belonging. 

“Not in the house,” insisted my wife, who was suffering with the flu. Baby heard her, ran in, and cuddled on her chest. A day later, my wife said, “Don’t leave her outside!”

We hadn’t thought to pick a name. 

“Come over here, baby.” 

“Want a treat, baby?”

“Where y’at, baby?”

She effectively named herself.

That first winter was cold, and she cuddled under the covers with us. Laid down with her head on a lap, gently nudging for ear pets. Watched cat-oriented videos of birds and squirrels, dashing behind the tablet. Bit the phone when we watched cat videos, defensive of her beloved humans.

Last night, she called a cuddle time-out. My wife is staying with a friend whose husband is in and out of hospital. Baby misses her. Meanwhile, my sewing hobby is taking on a life of its own, requiring regular trips to town for material and supplies. She’s noticed.[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!f452!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F97e778dd-d255-49c6-b390-a2bad2d73ac5_2328x1580.jpeg)

I was reviewing a new Wizards of the Coast product to blog about it. Had barely started a solo dungeon (quite possible with this deck) when Baby stood up on my bed. She leaned into my space at the desk as if to clear her throat.

I put the product back in its box and lay down with her on the bed. We watched Apple TV and YouTube shorts and played solitaire together. She gave me little baths and nudged for pets and eventually went to sleep with seventy percent of her body touching me, just to make sure I didn’t leave again. 

I’m going nowhere today unless I absolutely must. “I’m sorry, my cat needs me and I can’t come down.”
