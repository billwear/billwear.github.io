We live in a world that wants all of your attention, and it’s willing to lie, flatter, enrage, and exhaust you to get it.  Every click, every scene, and every AI chat-buddy response is designed to capture your mindshare while indoctrinating you with bad habits and potentially toxic ideas.  

So here’s my counter-spell: A serious pledge to declare independence from the hunter-gatherers clawing at our spirits.  It’s rather long, so I’ll break this into several posts. 

Think of it as a call to action.

## i declare the right to speak my mind

You may or may not be interested in my thoughts, my ways, or my morals.  But that doesn’t stop me from sharing them. One of the worst and most toxic sins against ourselves is to withhold our true opinions out of a desire to be liked and included.  If you think it through, though, being included in a crowd that doesn’t fit your true beliefs is worse than being alone.  Similarly, I expect that you will share your real thoughts with me, especially since I may not try to guess what you’re feeling.

> 

**Say what you mean; because telepathy is still in Beta.**

## i declare the right to curate my own mind

I want to cultivate, defend, and encourage the habit of sifting and filtering the mountain of information that assaults us daily.  Nothing could be more useful to one’s mental health than refusing entry to things that are wrong, toxic, or unworthy of our attention.  Likewise, nothing could be more healing than spending time with someone who has mastered a craft and gently shares it with others.

> 

**Be choosy with your media, because your brain takes it on faith.  Unless, of course, you’re watching someone paint happy little trees.**

## i declare the right to be mindful 

Everything around us has lessons to teach if we delay interpretation and take in what what actually happened.  I will not read things into what you say, nor will I cloak my own meaning.  Life in general is far too political to add to the mayhem.  Expect me to listen very carefully to what you say and take you at your word.  Expect me to ask about hints or nuances, rather than drawing conclusions.  And expect me to speak very clearly and cleanly.  

> 

**Speak clearly, listen carefully, and pay close attention.  Otherwise, we may end up in a conversation about quantum physics when all you wanted was the time.**

## i declare the right to pursue my own interests

Everyone has their gifts, but not everyone has developed them, or even acknowledged the beautiful contributions hidden inside them.  I’ve decided to claim my preferred crafts as sacred, irrespective of how well they sell.  Using your gifts isn’t just permitted, it’s imperative, and those weird, beautiful, useful things you make — that really matter to you — will matter to someone else, too.  Not because they’ll turn a profit, or because they’ll upscale, but trending beauty and solid crafting is a far better use of social media. 

> 

**Do what you love; life’s too short not to ply your craft.  Joy isn’t a business model, it’s a birthright.**

## i declare the right to build slowly and honestly, without shame

No more big launches or perfect plans that never get started.  I’m done with the hustle culture’s addiction to grand reinvention.  I’d rather make one true, rare, and valuable thing — slowly — than ten hollows ones quickly.  Progress is a ladder of real choices, not a dopamine rush.

> 

**Start small and build a little at a time.  A mosaic is more beautiful than the finest concrete, and less likely to get you sued for improper construction.**
