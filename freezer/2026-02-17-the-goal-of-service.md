At the moment, the world is chaotic, unprincipled, and disruptive, but these are just words, and they fall short. Real leaders always ride at the front issues directly: the needs and wishes and desires of the many, within the strong boundaries of a moral framework.

The current situation is just more proof that constraint works, while restraint mostly fails. That principle is at the heart of the fundamental documents of many modern nations. Populism seeks to use an ancient battle strategy to work around that: divide and conquer. Those who follow will obey without question, and work to marginalize or even destroy dissenters. This silly cosplay mocks 1 Corinthians 14:33: “God is not the author of chaos, so if you disagree with me, you’re serving Satan.”

There are at least two things wrong there: vilifying contradictory ideas, and ascribing to the devil what humans do quite well with no supernatural help whatsoever. These are the hallmarks of partisanship, the primary tool of corruption.

---

I’ll be centered on my own credo, thank you. I’m perfectly comfortable with moral ambiguity while following a precise and very accurate moral compass. I know right from wrong. If you read the Bible, we all do: what was that tree called again?  

There is righteousness, but also jurisdiction, the place where my rights cede to yours, and scope, beyond which I don’t care, because it’s outside my reach and none of my business. Entire generations comprehend none of this distinction, which is why they feel compelled to destroy any “dangerous” contradictory ideas. 

So, yeah, I’m in a mood right now. I’m feeling certainty: moral, religious, and political. I am confident amid chaos, mixed with equal parts disgust and revulsion. If that’s not a mood, I’ve never had one.

---

Yes, I come from an older generation. Labor makes me more tired and more sore for a longer time, but not that much. I’m aging very gracefully and gradually. Hard to be proud of genetics, but I’m happy to have them. I can still pick up my world by a corner and shake it out.

Energy and lethargy are partly physical but mostly mental for me. Ideas and desires are like stars: it takes a while to fuse the heavy metals required for rare and valuable work. Practice may appear to others to be inertia or even laziness. I prefer practice. Soon I’ll find the sweet spot and move on to the next rung. Even with age, there’s always a next rung.

Clear thinking is a peaceful and welcome escape from storms of emotion. Serenity is an active effort, but worthy. If anything, at this moment, I am mindfulness in motion. Let the bitter children fight; I have real things to do.

Winter and politicians can plan aggressively, but the earth has other plans. If anything, this is a season of wishful, greedy thinking, and too many of us forget that seasons change. 

I need to remember a sweater and tolerance when I go out. Nothing more nor less important than that.

---

In spite of the volatility of our current zeitgeist — this bitter mockery of  Shakespeare’s undiscovered and melancholy country — in my heart, deep triumph echoes.  Devotion to my hobbies is developing surgical precision with practice, tiny quantum leaps, heralded by both satisfaction and money, of all things.

I’m noticing that I’m noticing more things. Pushing aside questionable choices and gently choosing real righteousness never felt better. No willpower required, just quiet certainty. The barnacles of distracted living aren’t holding on.  Catching myself very early in the accidental dopamine spirals — and escaping clean — is a much bigger dopamine rush.

There are still shoulds and musts. Those never go away, but we have automation for that now. The daily noise is literally drowning in my sterile field of quiet attention.  And I’m not forgetting anyone. Suffering friends are never far from my heart. No list or pinging reminders needed.

Things still need doing, but they clarify themselves with less mental effort, or none at all.  Urgency still matters, but only within its particular proportions. No flashpoints here. I hesitate to say that I’ve found my exact center of gravity, but today the margin of error has ebbed considerably. 

---

From here, I can focus on big goals: End hunger. Negotiate world peace. Cure cancer. Not all at once, but I can make sure others get lunch; calm things down around me; and choose to model more healthy behaviors. Take the biggest step you can consistently, and your stride gradually improves, until mountains aren’t such obstacles.

Never mind the news cycle. My mechanic has gone silent on the car he’s had for a week now. My water bill is overdue. My writing is rejuvenated and ready to begin again. My sewing is becoming more precise and accurate, with my best attention to detail ever, for me. I’ll need to renew my rental car, most likely. There are people who might have a better day if I reached out. These are my world-changing shoulds.

---

Sewing is my compassion, my very own happy little trees. Speaking my mind gives me an avenue to share some peace and clarity, and speak in praise of volatile, bitter emotions sublimated into rare and valuable results. I might do better, but only with marginal returns. 

No magic beans, just commitment and good note-taking. My clearly alien brain, which offers no sense of direction because it has no left or right sides, is much happier tethered to mindfulness, clarity, and peace. But if we’re being honest, I really think human minds are like that, too.

When I avoid the social currency of cultivated outrage and the infinite spectrum of disunity and otherness, I’m not resisting anything that really matters. I’ll worry about the things you can’t kill by ignoring, like the dishes or healthy choices or putting my talents to work. 

---

Go ahead with your divisive rhetoric. If you need me, I’ll just be over here feeding the hungry, making friends with strangers, and visiting people in the hospital. Or maybe just smiling at the next person I pass. See Matthew 25, which is good advice, even for atheists.

If you’re having trouble remembering how, get in touch. I’m probably going to write a book about it, if for no other reason than to cut down on all the email that I struggle to answer.

Burn slow. Build deep. Be the proof.
