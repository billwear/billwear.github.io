The cat came back at dawn. No preamble this time. No slow inspection.  
Just a silent jump onto the counter and that pressure in the skull again — different frequency, tighter beam.

She wasn’t hungry. That was the first tell.  
The second was the scent-pattern: her tail held low, broadcasting a subharmonic I’d only felt around damaged warp-baffles and places where the geometry didn’t stay put.

Then the thought arrived.  
Sharper than yesterday. Fully formed.

**You’re not from here. Offworld.  
Something is wrong with your field.**

She didn’t push it with force.  
Just slid it into the cognitive channel the way an expert locksmith slides a pick into a lock that already wants to open.

Most humans wouldn’t feel the residue.  
Not without the training.  
Not without the glands.  
But she felt it.

The distortion still clinging to me — the microscopic phase grain the accident left behind — she sniffed it out like smoke on clothes. She circled once, slow, tail curled like a question mark made of suspicion.

I tried to respond verbally, but she shut it down with a single pulse:  
**Don’t talk. Just listen.**

The next thought carried weight.  
Old weight. Embers of something territorial and dangerous.

**There’s something here. On this land.  
Not from here. Not like you.  
Not safe.**

She projected an image pattern, but it broke apart halfway through — static, noise, angles that didn’t line up. Could’ve been a structure. Could’ve been a wound in the matter. Could’ve been a creature.

Hard to tell.  
Whatever it was, she didn’t want to touch it directly.

She pushed one more message — heavy, urgent, translated through instinct instead of language:

**It wakes sometimes.  
And it remembers.  
Be careful.**

Then she hopped off the counter, flicked her tail, and walked out into the morning like nothing had happened.

Left me standing there with a half-built Emacs function, a cold cup of coffee, and the sinking feeling that I’m not the only anomaly out here.

And whatever else is on this land?

It sensed me too.
