Today’s post breaks the rotation a little, on purpose.  But there *is* some sewing at the end, so read on!

I said Mondays would be sewing. And they will be, but not a lot of sewing today. 

Because before the work, there’s the structure that makes the work possible. And I want to talk about what I’m experimenting with right now, while it’s still alive and honest, not after it’s calcified into advice.

For a long time, I tried to schedule *results*. Finish this. Ship that. Make visible progress every day. It looks responsible on paper, but it quietly trains you to fight yourself. Miss a day and suddenly the system feels like an accusation instead of support.

## Habit slots

So I’m changing the unit of planning.

Instead of scheduling outcomes, I’m scheduling **habit slots**. Time blocks that say, “this kind of work lives here,” without demanding that anything specific be produced. Writing has a place. Sewing has a place. Electronics, programming, radio, fiction, all have a place too. Not because they all deserve equal weight, but because they all deserve *a home*.[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!exws!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F60973b3c-f044-43f0-a22d-4d72236a96f2_3456x2234.heic)

Some of these things will earn more than others. That’s real. Writing and sewing will likely carry more financial weight, and they show up more consistently through the week. The others are allowed to gather in a single day, or flare briefly, or go quiet for a while. This isn’t about equality. It’s about sustainability.[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IVau!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd495e7be-57e5-403b-b985-a2ac65c34b26_3456x2234.heic)

You’ll notice the schedule isn’t packed tight. Wednesday night ends early because church lives there. Saturday and Sunday trail off unfinished. That’s intentional. Those open edges aren’t laziness. They’re margin. They’re recovery. They’re room for life to happen without breaking the whole structure.

## Midnight snacks

There’s also a strange little slot around 3 a.m.

I wake up then most nights anyway. Always have. When it happens, I sometimes jot down an emologent or a rough blog shard. Not to publish. Not to polish. Just to empty the buffer so my mind can settle again. If I don’t wake up, or I don’t feel like it, I sleep. That’s the rule. Obligation would kill the usefulness of it.

This whole setup is an experiment. No vows. No streaks. No moral weight. If it works, I’ll keep what works. If parts of it don’t, I’ll change them. The goal isn’t control. It’s cooperation.

## For you
- 

**Schedule the kind of work, not the result.**  
Decide what you show up *as*, not what you must finish.
- 

**Lower the bar until showing up feels obvious.**  
If the slot feels heavy, it is too ambitious.
- 

**Let time blocks be invitations, not commands.**  
A habit that feels optional gets repeated more often.
- 

**Design for the day you actually have.**  
Leave space where energy predictably fades.
- 

**Capture thoughts when they surface, briefly.**  
Write just enough to release them, then stop.
- 

**Make skipping part of the system.**  
A habit that survives missed days is a real habit.
- 

**Vote for the person you want to be, quietly.**  
Every gentle repetition counts more than intensity.
