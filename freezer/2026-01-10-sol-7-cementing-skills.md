**Sol 7: Cementing skills**

Today, I’m diverting from my instrument pouch building and trying something a little simpler. I have this little night-time first-aid kit for little things, like an itch that won’t go away, heartburn, a headache, or just where the cat might have poked me with a claw while making biscuits. Keeping this in my nightstand drawer means “no getting up” when these things happen.

Currently, it’s in an old prototype phone case from years ago, when I first started to sew. Amateurish, but it’s functional, so I’ve been using it for a while.[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jw-2!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa98d8379-8656-4b90-940e-bb973b9b66a3.heic)

But now that I’m beginning to master gussets, I see an opportunity to make a better one, and work on inside pockets at the same time (yeah, I never go slow).

## **Sticking with the process**

First thing is to get a measurement, because this pouch is almost working — just needs a gusset and four inside pockets: one on each gusset for two tubular items (pill bottle and itch stick), and one each on front (bandaids, gauze), and back (alcohol pads, Neosporin, triple antibiotic packs). The heartburn meds can fit in the center of the pouch, along with other meds, if I decide to add them.

The lovely iPhone measurement camera tells me this:[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dxpV!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F63140b05-6382-48bf-b5d6-54cd186da14b_1021x380.jpeg)

So what would be something like the following:
- 

6” x 3” finished size
- 

3” flap (with a simple closure of some kind)
- 

1” wide finished gusset
- 

1.25” wide inside gusset pockets (2)
- 

3.25” wide inside pockets (2)

So the cut list looks like this:
- 

11” x 4” back piece
- 

7” x 4” front piece
- 

2” wide x 15” long gusset
- 

2.25” wide x 7” long gusset pockets (2)
- 

4.25” wide x 7” long pockets (2)

That’s it. Not a complex cut, just need to choose some decent material.
