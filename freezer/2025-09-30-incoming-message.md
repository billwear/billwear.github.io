This morning feels like trying to divide by zero. Undefined, yes, but not empty. No neat answer, no tidy quotient. Only suspension, a strange levelness, as if the ground has quietly shifted beneath me. The old reservations are still there, but faint, like outlines fading into mist. They are no longer in control.

The surface is restless. I woke too early. The cat prowled through half-formed dreams, crying because a baby lizard was on the floor and she didn’t know what to do. All my fancy technology refused to cooperate. My Emacs configuration blew apart, scattering text like leaves in a sudden gust. I had to strip it back to defaults, a reset I never asked for. False signal, false lift. Static in every direction. Was God shaking the snow globe?

But division by zero is not the end. It is the threshold. What looks like emptiness may be the beginning of signal, the moment before the carrier wave declares itself. A carrier wave doesn’t shout. It doesn’t even speak. It feels like someone pressing the microphone button without saying a word — holding the channel open, pressing back the static, preparing the air for a voice to enter.

At first you wonder: *is this thing even on?* But the longer you listen, the more you hear it. The quiet hum, the steady presence. Beneath the noise, beneath the scatter of code and thought, the wave holds.

The pivot is to notice. To name the shift without surrendering to it. To remember that static is not silence, and silence is not absence. The wave keeps transmitting whether I tune in or not.

And here is the rare value of the morning: undefined is not broken. Static is not the end of sound. Sometimes the noise drops away so completely it feels like nothing, but that nothing is actually the carrier, alive and waiting for a message to begin,

## For you

When your day feels scrambled but stable, when the lines won’t align, when life sounds like a false signal, remember this:
- 

**Don’t fight the undefined.** What feels empty may be recalibration. Let the silence prepare the signal.
- 

**Ground through the body.** A walk, a warm drink, a steady breath. The body knows how to tune back to the wave.
- 

**Name what still holds.** The keys still type. The light still shines. The breath still comes. Constants can carry you.
- 

**Listen for the carrier.** Static can scatter, but the wave is steady underneath. Always on, always present.
- 

**Don’t touch the dial**. A message is coming through: be prepared to listen and copy it down,

The inner fire does not always roar. Sometimes it hums like a carrier wave — quiet, steady, pressing back the static. Sometimes it scatters your day like broken code. Sometimes it shakes your world like a snow globe in God’s hand. But always, beneath it all, the wave holds. It has always been on.

And a message is coming.
