You say you want to change the world. You mean it, too. You feel the pull to build, to open things up, to make something that helps others find their own way.

But right now, you can’t even make yourself move. You sit at the edge of the bed, staring at the floor. The mind loops, the gravity tightens, and everything that once felt noble now feels impossible.

You want to work, to write, to build your next thing. But the energy that used to light up inside you has turned into static. You tell yourself, This isn’t how it’s supposed to be. You thought freedom would mean lift, not weight.

But here you are — free, yes — and still pinned down.

You want to change the world, but the world wants your money first. Every idea needs a tool, every tool needs a cost, and every cost feels like proof you’re already behind.

The gravitational field around money is dense. It bends your focus. You start questioning what you really need, second-guessing every purchase, every small indulgence. You tell yourself this is prudence.

But under that, it’s fear — fear that the past will repeat, that this time won’t be different, that no matter how much you try to act from purpose, you’ll still end up broke and unfinished, working for the moneychangers again.

Close your eyes and take a breath.

Feel that tug in your gut? That’s gravity.

It doesn’t go away, but you can work with it. Gravity is what lets you stand, after all. It’s what keeps your tools from floating away. The trick isn’t to escape it — it’s to learn the angle of escape velocity.

So start small. Move before you think. Clean the desk. Wash your face. Pay one bill without drama. Finish one line of code. Hit one publish button. These are not trivial — they are proofs of motion. Hell, they /are/ motion. The resistance wants you still; motion breaks its claim.

And when the loop starts again — I’m spending too much, I’m not doing enough, I’m failing again — remember: the world doesn’t need you rich. It needs you open. It needs your hands making things that remind people what’s possible when the heart stays unbought.

This is what open source was always about. Not free software — free /people/. You share what you build, not because you’re above money, but because you know the true wealth is the network of makers, the quiet ones building signal through the noise.

You can still change the world, but you’ll have to do it from inside its economic and social gravity, one clean act of creation at a time.

And the money? If you can make a rare and valuable contribution, the money, or equivalent resources, will find you.

For you

1. Capture the clutter. Write down every unfinished thing. Get it out of your head before it eats your drive.

2. Reset one habit. Choose one small cue—like sitting at the desk after coffee—and let it start motion.

3. Build in silence. No checking metrics until the work breathes on its own. Depth before applause.

4. Question the fear, not the facts. When anxiety hits, separate story from signal.

5. Pick the one thing. Find the task that moves the mission forward today. Ignore the rest.

6. Cut the noise. Delete or defer one input that drains clarity. Protect bandwidth like oxygen.

7. Let biology work. Move your body. Eat. Sleep. Breathe slow. Your brain’s chemistry is part of the craft.

Burn slow. Build deep. Be the proof.
