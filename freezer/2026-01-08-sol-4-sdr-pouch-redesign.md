# **The times, they are a-changing….**

My first step is deciding what to change. I don’t have time or money to be pedantic and change just one thing when there are several things I see, so I’m going to make more than one update at once:
- 

**Change fabric to white canvas**. Very NASA-esque. The denim idea is cute, but thick and hard to sew.
- 

**Hide all seams, even the turning seam**. This remnant was already cut too close to the pocket to do that.
- 

**Make the pocket square. **The angled bottom is fashionable but wasted space.
- 

**Give the pocket some sides**. I have two different kinds of framework material that will give me a stiff, rectangular pocket opening. It will be easier to get the SDR kit in and out.
- 

**Make a smaller duplicate when done, with a belt loop on the back**. I can use this to carry my iPhone, since I have two portable phones now.

# **Laying out the design**

Let’s try another sketch, but this time with some attention to measurements. It looks like the best arrangement is three pockets 3” wide by 8” tall by 1.5 “ deep, and a 12” wide slip pocket along the bottom for the whip antenna.[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!duKM!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2f801e57-04b8-4844-ade6-974ac72d64f9_2954x2358.png)

## **Layout advice (fabric-efficient)**

If canvas is standard **60” wide** (like mine is):
- 

Backing panel goes first (15” x 12”)
- 

Pocket faces can be strip-cut:
- 

cut a 9” tall strip
- 

sub-cut into four 4” widths
- 

Gussets:
- 

cut long 2.5” strips, then sub-cut to 19”
- 

Whip pocket pieces come from strip leftovers easily

This design should nest cleanly with minimal waste.

## **Construction order**

Without pattern instructions, making a quick list is usually sanity-preserving for me.
- 

Build each gusseted vertical pocket as a unit (outer + lining)
- 

Attach pockets to backing (center two first, then outer two)
- 

Add whip antenna pocket last
- 

Close backing sandwich
