Not every gift arrives in quiet. Some do. Some slip into the world like morning light on still water, a gentle reflection you hardly notice until it warms your face.

But some gifts come as storms.

There are days when the gift feels like fire, like gale-force winds tearing across continents. An unsettled mood, fierce and unrepentant. Everything seems wrong and bent, an ocean of disarray and disrepair. Nothing invites compromise. The spirit surges like a weather front, hunting for what is true and right and good.

The weather of the mind is not always peaceful. Sometimes it is hurricanes and tornadoes, lightning splitting the sky, and volcanoes erupting with molten force. Sometimes it is earthquakes that laugh at our silly, fragile human caste society.  Sometimes it is solar energy gone wild. We are unconstrained, irresistible, and highly intolerant to nonsense.

That, too, is a gift.

No, it will not be polite. It does not ask permission. But its very value lies in this force.

It clears paths no one else dares to walk. It pushes back boundaries no one else will touch. Most importantly, it confronts the lies no one else is willing to face.

You might think such force is reckless. And without grounding, it can be. But the carrier is boosted. The signal is borne by God himself, the One who drives the wind, pulls the rain, refreshes the earth, resets our minds. Power without purpose is destruction; power carried by God becomes creation renewed.

This is not chaos. This is cleansing. Not proof of self-sufficiency, but proof of image. We are already enough because we are made in His likeness. Even our fire is a reflection of the Creator — the One who spoke light into darkness, split seas, shook mountains, and makes all things new.

Your gift is the message, yes. But the message does not always come as just a still, small voice. Like it came Elijah in the cave on Mount Horeb, sometimes it starts in wind and thunder. Sometimes it shakes the ground and topples what cannot stand, so that what is true and good may remain. Most often, it starts as an Inner Fire.

Transmuting power

Raw force is never the finish line. Lightning without a channel is a wildfire. Earthquakes without design leave only rubble. Fire has to be transmuted into warmth, into light, into fuel.

The storm within must learn its own discipline. Energy must be metered, aimed, released with care. Otherwise it becomes noise. Harnessed, it becomes revolution.

This is why the old comic book line still matters: with great power comes great responsibility. If you have been given storm-energy, then your responsibility is to direct it, not to squander it. To strike with clarity, not abandon. To change the world, not simply scorch it.

Your fire is not for spectacle. It is for service.

And sometimes, after the storm, comes the slow burn. The sun itself is fire — but disciplined, measured, steady across millennia. That slow burn gives life, warmth, and direction without collapse.

Our storm-gifts are not meant to rage forever. They are meant to clear the air, then settle into a lasting radiance. That was the lesson of Slow Burn, and it belongs here as well: sometimes the gift is not thunder but enduring flame. Sometimes it is both. The task is the same — to turn raw fire into a source of life.

For you

So what do you do with this kind of energy — this impatience, this storm? Here are six anchors:

1. Ground yourself daily. Prayer, stillness, journaling, breath — storms without grounding uproot; storms with grounding irrigate. Set aside time when your heart is most malleable and your mind most reflective, in a place that gives you hope, and reflect.

2. Name the target. Anger without focus scatters. Decide: this is the wall that must come down, this is the injustice that must be named, this is the problem that must be solved. Aim before you fire, and then pour all your energy into the shot.

3. Meter the force. Not every moment deserves the full gale. Deliver in pulses, in chosen battles. A storm that never relents becomes background noise; a storm that chooses its timing changes history. Learn to harness and harbor your anger and drive. Martin Luther King, Jr., said that when he was angry, he could preach well and pray well. Learn what that means.

4. Refuse carelessness. Power tempts wild swings. Resist. Make every blow count. Every word, every act, delivered with pinpoint precision. Open flame flickers; a lantern shows the path. The super-focused light of a laser cuts through obstacles with finesse. Find the one thing that needs changing and put your raw energy behind changing it.

5. Transmute, don’t just release. Let the energy become creation: write, build, design, speak, protest, heal. Release may feel good in the moment, but transmutation leaves fruit behind. Let your gift be used in service to the truth, knowing that your raw energy will drive you to do great things.

6. Stay tethered to love. Fire without love destroys. Fire with love purifies. Remember always: you burn not to ruin, but to renew; not to punish, but to teach; not merely to gain attention, but to draw attention to what matters.  

Serve the greater good

As Paul said in Philippians 4:8 (kjv)

"Finally, brethren, whatsoever things are true, whatsoever things are honest, whatsoever things are just, whatsoever things are pure, whatsoever things are lovely, whatsoever things are of good report; if there be any virtue, and if there be any praise, think on these things."

And I say, when you think of them -- when you find them -- put your power behind them. When the gift comes as fire, don’t fear it. Don’t apologize for it. Harness it. Ground it. Aim it. Transmute it. Drive the world that much closer to truth, justice, clarity, and beauty.  Do things that touch the future.

Let God carry the signal, and your storm will not only shake the earth — it will renew it.
