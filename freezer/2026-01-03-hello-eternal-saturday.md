## Mood

My mood is good today, powerful even, for the first time in a really long time. I am in my element, making a logical and natural transition to a more creative posture. Less configuring, more doing; no excuses, few distractions.

## Energy

My energy is decent, even if I did get a slow start today. I enjoyed sleeping in, since I was just for ninety minutes around 3:30 AM. I’m moving my bedtime to midnight, which feels more natural for my circadian rhythms. I’ll probably get up when I get up, unless I have something scheduled. That should create some improvements in my energy.  
  
And my sore throat is finally starting to improve. A couple of weeks ago, I got an anti-viral nasal spray. Didn’t really touch it. Wednesday I went back and got antibiotics, and they seem to be slowly starting to create healing. It’s the third day and they seem to be kicking in. Besides, I don’t want to feed hypochondria just because my worries are much less these days.

## Mental Weather

Things in my head are suitably swirly, and for once, I’m actually okay with that. Clouds? No problem. Rainy mood? Good, we can use that, it’s great writer’s energy. Foggy? Nice. I learn things when my visibility is limited.

## The Actual Weather

At the moment, it’s rainy, but the weather has been schizophrenic and lagging the forecast by quite a bit. Today it started raining just when my weather app notified me that “thunderstorms should stop soon.” Weather forecaster is the only job where you can be wrong. Every. Single. Day. And still get raises and promotions. It’s okay, though: I have no outside plans, really, and no big need for any particular kind of weather, as long as it’s not threatening.

## The Loop

I’m deep in retraining mode. I bought a fly MacBook Pro yesterday. It was partly whim and partly just creative logic. I have way too many creative pursuits and no time for maintaining infrastructure on the run. Lots of things I want to actually get done.   
  
Yes, it was a big expense, but the numbers are working for me: social security, medicare and private insurance for my partner cover my basic expenses plus normal entertainment (TV, eating out now and again, etc.). What I need to make up is “mad money” so we can enjoy the lifestyle we prefer, and get upgrades as needed.   
  
This mad money is slated to come from my creative efforts: sewing, writing, D&D, electronics, radio, coding, OS & networking stuff. These are all very good bets, and the Mac is the perfect tool. Hence the loop is settling. And Ubuntu and Emacs? Well, about that....

## Lingering

So mostly what’s hanging around in my head are actual things that I should actually do, not tweaks I need to make to the stone knives and bearskins I was using to be able to get things done. So far, I’ve either bought outright or subscribed to OmniFocus (GTD on steroids), Scrivener, Ulysses, Xmind, and Day One. This plus what’s actually already on the Mac fills all the gaps in my seven-book utility belt, the one I built last summer. These tools give me the best possible shot at achieving my “goals” for 2026.

## Gravity

So it’s time to get to work: no over-planning, overthinking, or tool-building. Dump all the prep work I felt like I had to do as a corporate employee. It’s actually nice to think about the future again. I needed this.

## Anchor

> 

My bedrock is my emologent. It sets my day spinning up with the right tone, attitude, and expectations; relieves the pressure to perform; and helps me sublimate my negativity, fear, and weird issues into something that can be used. That’s great for writers, because negative emotions have as much value (if not more so) than positive ones.

I’ll have to trick her while she’s eating in the morning, I guess. At least she’ll give me a few minutes’ peace to finish this: She’s been needy since Mommy is staying with a sick friend this week.*

## Impedance Matching

Oh, my Lord! The Mac/Apple kit is *sooo* much better. It fits like a glove; the tools are just what they need to be. Ah, the years I wasted writing mountains of eLisp to get Linux to do this. Don’t get me wrong, it’s still great for coding. But it’s weird making the change: you’d think it would be jarring, but I don’t actually miss the old ways at all. So much motion for such meager results. 

## Idea Pressure

Lately, it feels like an eternal Saturday. Well, I mean, there’s Sunday, which has church, which sort of punctuates the week, but you know what I mean. And all of the pressure is not about work, but about intense devotion. Hobbies and avocations is probably where I wanted to be, and definitely where I need to be spending my time.

## Creative Current

What a list! I’m getting better with my sewing. I’ve given no pretense about early quality, and set prices accordingly. People are still buying stuff pretty quick, especially the mug rugs. Maybe that’s because everything is so expensive right now? I know when I checked my grocery receipts, the prices of almost everything are up 135%. That’s not inflation or tariffs or anything but just opportunistic greed. Maybe I can be a place where people can get a little flair or style in their life without having to pay insane prices.  
  
I’ve also got some interest in sewing some D&D-theme stuff for hire: RenFaire & cosplay costumes, bags, belts, and accessories. I’ll take it. And I also plan to get more heavily into D&D. Stranger Things has got lots of people wanting to play Dungeons and Dragons. Gotta ride that wave while it’s out there and see how far we can carry it. On that note, I also have an idea for a D&D campaign called L’Ethos, where ethical decision-making is the only way to win.   
  
There’s also electronics and shortwave radio. Certainly some of that is teaching, but most of it is doing things in public, as my friend Sally says. And building neat stuff that people may want curated kits for, apparently that’s a big thing, too. And I have five books I want to write. Gonna write ‘em anyway, maybe submit, maybe self-publish on Amazon. Dunno.  
  
And this newsletter / blog / substack / whatever it is.

## **Pivot**

Who knew that this Mac would be such a big turning point. Yes, it cost a bundle, I admit, but look at the returns already. 

## Signal-to-Noise Ratio

Signal up, noise way down, no doubt. Seems like it should be hard to accept, because I’ve given half my life to Emacs. But just because I’ve always done it some other way doesn’t mean I should keep doing it that way. And the ease with which I transitioned told me the new way is a much better way for me. Are there regrets about taking so long? I’ll let you know if I have any. Don’t hold your breath.

## Rare Value

It’s a meta-thing, I guess: figuring myself out in public, being open with my struggles and doing extensive self-analysis in plain view. The rare part is being unafraid to do it this way. Some may make fun of me; if I catch them, I’ll have just two words for them, and you know which two those are. Now I just need to make this newfound energy valuable.

## Points of Friction

So it’s gone from darkness to discovery to a kid-in-the-candy-store problem in just a half a year. Where do I start? Who’s are first? What are reasonable linkages between hobbies, so they can feed off of each other? No rush, just continuous devotion, I guess.

## Quest Pulse

I’m starting the engine, finally. It happens to be handy to do now, at the beginning of the year, and it will definitely need a little throttling to keep the speed up, but it feels really good.
