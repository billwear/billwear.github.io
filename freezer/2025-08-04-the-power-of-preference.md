*Changing through gentle persuasion*

My psyche feels like a masterwork of contradiction: memories of actions and decisions, layered in a translucent patchwork of conflicting emotions.  The color changes with each layer, shifting virtually toward red or green, from which I choose “don’t” or “do.”  

Very bad people and very bad decisions can push me away for decades, while the emotional healing process tries to lay down as many layers of “it’s okay” as needed to put things right. Traumatic events and difficult circumstances cast a long, starkly fearful shadow that takes time to overcome. My heart must find its way back to trust, one restorative outcome at a time. 

I’ve found a way to speed this up which works for me. I’ve tentatively named it, “preferential mindfulness.” It feels like hard work, and it requires of me a sort of kaizen walk-in. Maybe, if you’re brave and a little adventurous, you can see whether or not it’s just me.Let me step you through it

At its heart, the concept is simple: just respond to your thoughts with a mental “like” or “dislike.”  That’s it.

Simple in concept, but requires some progressive effort in practice. I’m still a little torn on exactly how to begin, but let’s start with the heart’s desire to form habits. Start with an emotional inventory every morning, not long after you wake up:
- 

What’s your mood?
- 

Do you like it, or dislike it?
- 

What’s your energy level?
- 

Like or dislike?
- 

What’s your mental weather?
- 

Like or dislike?

You’re trying to build a backdoor habit of stating a mild preference about emotionally-charged thoughts. If you log it for a few weeks, your moods and energy level will improve -- if your feelings work like mine do.Add a physical trigger

After a while, start adding a physical trigger, something you can do unnoticed in every situation. In my case, that’s squeezing my first three fingers together on my right hand. 

Start by doing it while you’re thinking about your moods, energy level, and mental weather. Then start to repeat it anytime you’re feeling strong emotion, as a reminder to think to yourself, “like” or “dislike.” 

From there, just let the soulful machinery of mental habit take over, and tell me what happens.
