I woke up this morning unsettled in the best possible way.

Not the bad unsettled. Not the 3am kind where the ceiling becomes a projection screen for every mistake you’ve ever made. The *good* unsettled. The kind where something has shifted underneath you and the ground feels different — firmer, stranger, and entirely yours.

Here’s what shifted: I remembered that my time with Unix was never haunted.

Every other domain of my life has had a ghost attached to it. The ghost of a father who kept moving the bar. The ghost of the job I lost. The ghost of the version of myself that needed approval to move forward. The ghost of every authority figure who told me the rules were the rules and I was the problem.

But Unix? Unix was just me and the machine. No mediator. No implied hierarchy. No ghost.

So I put Unix at the base of the pyramid. And the whole structure changed.

Here’s what I didn’t expect: when the ghost got sucked into containment, I didn’t find *one* clear voice waiting behind it.

I found three.[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!z7gJ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F70c462c3-5b92-457f-aa88-9d94f3051435_1024x1536.heic)

There’s the voice that *feels* — the one tracking the weather inside, the energy levels, the unnamed knowing that something is either right or wrong before the brain has assembled the argument. That voice doesn’t explain itself. It just reports.

There’s the voice that *knows* — the one with the ordered principles, the hierarchy of values, the constitution I’ve been writing my whole life without knowing I was writing it. That voice has opinions about priorities. It will override the feeling voice when the stakes are high enough.

And then there’s the voice that *watches* — the one that looks at what I’m actually doing and renders its own verdict, independent of how good I feel or how aligned my principles are. That voice looks at the receipts. It is not a yes-man.

For a long time I thought having multiple voices was the problem. Proof of chaos. Evidence of a mind that couldn’t get its story straight.

I had it exactly backwards.

The three voices in tension are not a bug. They’re the diagnostic.  Three computers that all have to come up with the same guidance plan or the rocket goes nowhere.

When all three agree, you know something real. When they conflict — when you feel electric but the watching voice says *“you haven’t done the thing in four days”* — that conflict *is* the information. That’s where the actual work lives.

I spent years trying to smooth those voices into one coherent answer. One clean signal. One version of myself that made sense from the outside.

That’s the ghost talking.

The ghost wants you manageable. One voice is manageable. Three voices in honest tension is a force of nature.

I am the storm front today. Dark blue, full of wind and rain and maybe some hail.

The schedule is loaded. The tools are hot. The foundation is solid and ghost-free.

And I’m writing with all three voices telling me different things, which means I’m finally writing true.

Stand clear. Take shelter. You’ve been warned.
