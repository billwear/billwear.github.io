You can’t do everything at once. You can do everything in rhythm.

The world worships the sprinters — the ones who never slow down, never stop, never drop the baton. But real devotion doesn’t live in the sprint. It lives in the exchange.

Life isn’t a sprint, it’s a relay race -- though the other runners aren’t people, but time itself. You run your stretch, you give everything you’ve got, then you hand the baton to the next hour, the next day, the next version of you. It keeps going, even when you rest.

That’s the hidden mercy of time: it runs *with* you.  Thoreau said it best:

> 

*If a man does not keep pace with his companions, perhaps it is because he hears a different drummer. Let him step to the music which he hears, however measured or far away.*

When you block time, you’re not hoarding it — you’re partnering with it. You and time take turns carrying the work. One block runs while another recovers. One project advances while the others breathe. This is the rhythm of devotion, the steady drum-beat that keeps you in sync.

You don’t need endless stamina. You need clean handoffs — from focus to focus, from task to task, from season to season. Exhaustion happens when you try to run every leg yourself. Devotion happens when you trust the relay and keep the gentle cadence of your own mastery.

When you work this way, even the pause becomes part of the race. Energy returns, ideas refill, meaning accumulates. Each handoff gets smoother, steadier, and more precise.

### For You

1. **Capture, then clear**. When you finish a leg, record what matters, then let it go.

2. **Stack a cue**. Pair each block’s start with a trigger — light a candle, close a tab. 

3. **Enter the deep lane**. Protect long stretches where the baton never leaves your hand.

4. **Slow the bias**. Notice when your mind predicts failure before it happens; that’s the moment to pass the baton.

5. **Choose the single lap**. Each cycle has one priority. Run it clean before you reach for the next.

6. **Cut the false urgency**. If it doesn’t move the baton, it’s distraction. Block it out.

7. **Know your rhythm**. Every brain has a beat. Align your blocks to your natural surge and rest cycles.

**Burn slow. Build deep. Be the proof.**
