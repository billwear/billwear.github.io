Did my best to translate the Declaration of Independence into more modern language. 

Here’s a straight paraphrase into modern English. No flourish, no imitation of the original cadence, no patriotic hype. Just the plain meaning in today’s language. 

This isn't perfect, but it's probably worth a read.  After all, if we don't learn from history, how will we know whether or not we're repeating it?

---

### Declaration of Independence 

(modern language)

When a group of people decides they can’t stay politically tied to another group of people anymore, and they want to become independent, they should explain why. It’s basic respect for the rest of the world.

We believe some things are just true. People are created equal in moral worth. Everyone has certain rights that don’t come from a government and can’t be taken away — like life, freedom, and the chance to pursue what matters to them. Governments exist to protect those rights. Governments get their authority from the consent of the people they govern.

When a government stops protecting those rights and instead becomes a source of harm, the people have the right — and even the responsibility — to change it or replace it with something better. But this is not something to do casually. History shows that people usually put up with a lot rather than go through the upheaval of changing governments. Only when abuses pile up in a clear pattern, all pointing to the same purpose — putting people under absolute control — are they justified in breaking away.

That’s our situation now. The King of Great Britain has repeatedly acted in ways that violate our rights and undermine our self-government. Here are the main points:

- He has blocked laws we needed for the public good.

- He has prevented us from governing ourselves effectively.

- He has refused to pass laws unless we gave up our right to representation.

- He has obstructed immigration and naturalization.

- He has interfered with the functioning of our courts.

- He has made judges dependent on his approval for their jobs and pay.

- He has created new offices and sent swarms of officials to harass us.

- He has kept standing armies among us in peacetime without our consent.

- He has made the military independent of and superior to civilian authority.

- He has cut off our trade.

- He has imposed taxes on us without representation.

- He has denied us trial by jury.

- He has sent people overseas for trials.

- He has tried to impose laws on neighboring territories to use as a model for controlling us.

- He has encouraged internal rebellions and Native attacks on frontier settlements.

- He has called legislative bodies together in inconvenient places just to weaken their influence.

- He has repeatedly dissolved elected assemblies.

- He has ignored our attempts to establish new governments after causing political chaos.

- He has forced us to house troops.

- He has protected soldiers who harmed us from consequences.

- He has waged war against us.

- He has burned our towns, destroyed our ships, and killed our people.

We’ve tried repeatedly to resolve these issues peacefully. We’ve warned the British people, appealed to their sense of justice, and reminded them of our shared values. They haven’t listened. So we have no choice left.

Therefore, we — the representatives of the United States of America — declare that we are free and independent states. We’re cutting all political ties with Great Britain. As independent states, we claim the full powers any nation has: making war, building peace, creating alliances, and managing trade.

We do this with firm trust in divine justice, and we pledge to each other our lives, our fortunes, and our sacred honor.
